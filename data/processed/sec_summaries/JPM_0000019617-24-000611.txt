• **Net Interest Income (NII) Inflection:** FY24 NII guidance (ex-Markets) raised to ~$92.5B (up from ~$91B). Despite management's "conservative" rhetoric regarding rate sensitivity, the 10-Q reveals a slower-than-expected migration of non-interest-bearing deposits (-3% QoQ), suggesting a NIM floor is forming despite Fed easing.
• **Credit Normalization Divergence:** CCB Card Net Charge-offs (NCOs) accelerated to 2.81% (up 31bps QoQ). While management terms this "normalization," the 30-89 day delinquency bucket rose to 2.11%, signaling a potential overshoot of pre-pandemic loss rates by H1 2025.
• **Capital Allocation Acceleration:** Share repurchases increased to $6.0B in Q3 (vs. $4.9B in Q2), signaling high confidence in absorbing Basel III "Endgame" RWA inflation, which is now internally estimated at a manageable ~15% increase vs. prior ~25% fears.
• **Investment Banking (CIB) Pipeline:** Fee revenue rose 31% YoY. While "robust" backlog is cited, the 10-Q indicates a pivot toward DCM/ECM activity (up 56% and 26% respectively) as M&A remains structurally constrained by regulatory friction.
• **Operational Pivot:** Tech spend remains at a ~$17B annual run rate, but R&D intensity has shifted significantly toward GenAI productionization, with over 400 use cases now impacting front-office productivity metrics.
• **Asset Wealth Management (AWM) KPI:** Record $3.9T AUM; NRR (Net Revenue Rate) remains stable despite fee compression in passive products, offset by a +15% surge in alternative investment inflows.
• **Regulatory Risk:** Materially modified risk language regarding "G-SIB surcharge" recalibration and "Resolution Plan" requirements indicates higher-than-consensus operational overhead for the 2025-2026 cycle.
