• **Automotive Pivot:** Revenue surged 87% YoY to $811M, now representing 10% of QCT segment; signifies a shift from "design win" backlog to active revenue-generating deliveries and higher ASP via Digital Chassis integration.
• **IoT/AI Divergence:** IoT revenue remains in contraction at -8% YoY ($1.36B), contradicting management’s bullish "Edge AI" narrative; signals persistent inventory overhang in industrial/consumer channels despite AI-PC (Snapdragon X Elite) launches.
• **Guidance Risk Nuance:** Management used "expect" for Q4 revenue ($9.5B–$10.3B) but transitioned to "anticipate" regarding the impact of the US Department of Commerce revoking export licenses for a major Chinese customer (Huawei). This indicates lower confidence in mitigating the resultant revenue gap in FY25.
• **R&D Intensity:** R&D spend rose to $2.42B (26% of revenue), a 9% increase YoY; focuses heavily on NPU (Neural Processing Unit) architecture, signaling a resource shift away from legacy modem dominance toward high-compute PC and Auto silicon.
• **ARM Litigation:** Trial confirmed for December 2024. Management tone remains "speculative" (using "believe" vs. "expect") regarding the defense of Nuvia-derived IP, a critical threat to the Snapdragon X Series roadmap.
• **Capital Allocation:** Accelerated share repurchases to $1.3B in Q3 (vs. $1.1B in Q2); $1.7B remains in the current $10B authorization, suggesting potential for a new buyback program announcement in Q4 or Q1 FY25.
• **Margin Resilience:** QCT EBT margins expanded to 27% (up 300 bps YoY) despite flat unit volumes, driven almost exclusively by premium-tier handset mix (Snapdragon 8 Gen 3) and disciplined OpEx.
• **Inventory/Supply Chain:** Inventory levels stabilized at $6.23B; however, accounts receivable increased 15% QoQ to $4.0B, suggesting aggressive back-end loaded shipping or extended payment terms for key OEMs.
