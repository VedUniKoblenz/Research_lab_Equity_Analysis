• **Sentiment Divergence:** Management continues to cite "strong consumer balance sheets," yet the **Provision for Credit Losses surged to $1.1B** (vs. a $222M benefit in Q221). The $411M allowance build contradicts the "resilient consumer" narrative, signaling internal modeling of a significant macro-economic downturn.
• **Hidden Operational Shifts:** A strategic deprecation in the Auto segment is evident; **originations fell to $7.7B (down 17% YoY)** as management pivoted toward "disciplined pricing," effectively exiting lower-margin volume-chasing as used car ASPs soften.
• **Domestic Card Trajectory:** 30+ day delinquency rate hit **2.47% (+73bps YoY)**, while the net charge-off rate rose to 2.26%. The normalization is occurring faster than the 2019 baseline trajectory, specifically in the subprime/revolving segments.
• **NIM vs. Funding Costs:** Net Interest Margin (NIM) expanded to **6.76% (+11bps QoQ)**, but the 10-Q adds a material risk factor regarding "deposit beta acceleration" and competition, suggesting NIM may peak earlier than consensus expectations.
• **Capital Allocation Brake:** The **CET1 ratio fell to 12.1%** (from 12.7% in Q122). Because the company is nearing its 11% target, share buybacks were aggressively throttled—**$7.5M repurchased in Q2 vs. $2.0B in Q1**, a 99% reduction in pace.
• **Commercial Segment Risk:** While NPLs remain low, the **ACL coverage for Commercial & Multifamily Real Estate rose to 1.34%**, indicating a preemptive hedge against CRE volatility not yet reflected in current defaults.
• **Efficiency Floor:** Non-interest expenses hit **$4.7B (+14% YoY)**. Management "anticipates" high tech spending will persist, making the 53.9% efficiency ratio difficult to compress in a slowing revenue environment.
