• **BDS Structural Margin Collapse**: Defense, Space & Security (BDS) reported a $(527)M Q2 loss, driven by aggressive reach-forward charges on fixed-price development contracts. Significant hits: MQ-25 ($527M) due to schedule delays/integration costs, Commercial Crew ($257M) following Starliner launch deferrals, and T-7A ($189M) on higher estimated production costs. Total BDS backlog stands at $58B, but 20% remains tied to these high-risk, fixed-price structures.
• **BCA Production Ramp vs. Liability Drag**: 737 production rate finalized move to 38/month; 787 rate increased to 5/month. Despite ramp, 737 inventory is heavily encumbered by $2.4B in accrued customer concessions. Abnormal costs for the 777X program reached $514M in Q2 ($1.0B YTD), expensed as incurred due to low production rates, with first delivery still speculative for 2025.
• **Inventory Quality Divergence**: Total inventory grew to $79.3B. Crucially, "Work in Process" includes $14.1B in deferred production costs for the 787 program. Management's "expectation" of 70-80 787 deliveries in 2023 is high-confidence, but relies on liquidating a stagnant parked fleet requiring extensive "rework." 
• **Service Segment Outperformance**: Global Services (BGS) remains the primary alpha driver with 18.0% operating margins ($856M earnings on $4.7B revenue), neutralizing BCA/BDS losses.
• **Capital Liquidity Profile**: Cash/Marketable securities at $13.8B vs. $52.3B total debt. Consolidated debt-to-equity remains meaningless due to $16.1B shareholders' deficit. No resumption of dividends or buybacks; capital allocation prioritized toward $5.2B R&D/Capex YTD.
