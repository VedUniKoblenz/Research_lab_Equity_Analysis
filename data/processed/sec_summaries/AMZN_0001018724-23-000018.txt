• **AWS Margin Inflection:** Operating margin expanded to 30.3% (from 24.2% in Q2), driven by headcount stabilization and cost-optimization cycles. AWS RPO (Remaining Performance Obligations) reached $133.0B, with a 30% YoY growth rate; notably, ~51% of this backlog is scheduled for recognition within the next 12 months, providing high-visibility ARR support.
• **Fulfillment Structural Pivot:** Transition from national to 8-region fulfillment model yielded a 19% YoY reduction in miles traveled and a 20% decrease in "touches" per package. Outsized leverage: Fulfillment costs as a percentage of sales declined despite $1.3B in incremental wage investments.
• **R&D/GenAI Capital Reallocation:** Technology and Infrastructure spend surged to $21.2B (+11% YoY). This reflects a stealth pivot from fulfillment capacity expansion toward generative AI (LLM training, Bedrock) and Kuiper satellite infrastructure. Management tone transitioned from "anticipating" AI demand to "expecting" (high confidence) multi-billion dollar AI revenues.
• **Working Capital Arbitrage:** Accounts Payable surged to $79.5B (+$7.0B QoQ) while Accounts Receivable only increased $4.0B. This $3B net float suggests aggressive vendor payment term extensions to fund capex without secondary debt issuance.
• **Capital Allocation & Debt:** Zero share repurchases in Q3 despite $10B remaining authorization. Focus shifted to servicing the $8.0B term loan (SOFR-linked) and integrating the $3.5B One Medical acquisition.
• **Risk Factor Escalation:** Materially modified language regarding regulatory exposure; specific emphasis on FTC and multi-state antitrust litigation ("Project Nessie") targeting Prime ecosystem and pricing algorithms, moving from speculative to active litigation risk.
• **Inventory Velocity:** Inventory levels held flat at $36.1B despite 13% YoY revenue growth, indicating significantly improved turnover and reduced write-down risk heading into Q4.
