• **Broadband Growth Inflection:** Net additions reached 0 in Q2 2022 (vs. 354k YoY), a critical pivot from historical growth trajectories. Management tone remains defensively bullish on "long-term penetration," yet raw data shows total customer relationship growth slowed to 1.3%.

• **Fixed Wireless (FWA) & Fiber Threat:** Risk disclosure now emphasizes "heightened competitive environment," specifically from fixed wireless providers and fiber overbuilders, signaling a shift from cyclical to structural headwinds in the Cable segment.

• **Wireless as Secondary Anchor:** Xfinity Mobile added 317k lines (penetration at ~10% of broadband base); management is increasingly reliant on wireless bundling to mitigate broadband churn.

• **NBCUniversal Margin Divergence:** Media EBITDA fell 7.4% to $1.3B due to Peacock losses ($467M in Q2, tracking toward $2.5B full-year loss). Conversely, Theme Parks EBITDA hit a record $632M, a 186% YoY increase, acting as a high-margin hedge against content spend.

• **Sky Segment Fragility:** Revenue decreased 13.8% (down 2.3% on FX-neutral basis). Direct-to-consumer subscribers fell by 255k, reflecting severe macroeconomic pressure and "cost of living" impacts in UK/European markets.

• **Capital Allocation Pace:** Share buybacks accelerated to $3.0B in Q2 ($6.0B YTD). Front-loading of the buyback program suggests a lack of M&A appetite at current valuations and a prioritization of floor-setting for the stock price.

• **Guidance Nuance:** "Expect" language shifted toward "operating efficiency" and "ARPU growth" (Cable ARPU +2.0%) rather than volume-based growth, signaling a transition to a "value-over-volume" strategy.
