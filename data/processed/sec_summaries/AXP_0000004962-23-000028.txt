• CREDIT RISK DIVERGENCE: Net write-off rates (principal only) for both Consumer and Small Business Cardmember Loans doubled YoY, reaching 1.8% in 1H23 vs 0.8% in 1H22. Including interest and fees, the Consumer net write-off rate escalated to 2.2%, signaling a rapid normalization of credit losses exceeding historical pacing.
• LOAN BOOK VELOCITY: Total Cardmember loans reached $117.1B, up 8.1% from $108.3B at FYE22. Growth is heavily skewed toward the Consumer segment ($81.7B) vs. Small Business ($23.1B), increasing portfolio sensitivity to individual employment and spend volatility.
• DELINQUENCY FORMATION: Consumer loans in the 30-59 days past due bucket jumped to $484M from $377M at FYE22 (+28.4%), providing a high-confidence lead indicator for rising charge-offs in Q3 and Q4. 90+ day delinquencies also rose to $331M.
• SECURITIZATION DEPENDENCY: Consolidated Variable Interest Entities (VIEs) now hold $21.4B in Cardmember loans and $19.0B in receivables, representing approximately 18% of the total loan book, signaling continued reliance on asset-backed markets for liquidity and capital relief.
• SEGMENT KPI (FINANCIALS): Interest income on Cardmember loans is the primary revenue driver, but the doubling of write-off rates suggests a tightening of the Net Interest Margin (NIM) on a risk-adjusted basis, as loss provisions offset yield gains.
• CAPITAL ALLOCATION: Retained earnings increased to $24.7B from $23.1B in Q1, indicating net income is currently outpacing share buybacks and dividends despite a Series D Preferred Stock par value of $1.667.
• RECEIVABLES CONCENTRATION: Corporate receivables remained stagnant at $21.9B compared to Consumer's $28.3B, marking a shift in operational focus away from commercial/T&E volume toward high-yield revolving consumer credit.
