• **Accounting Alpha/Sentiment Divergence:** Effective Jan 1, 2025, AMZN extended the estimated useful lives of technology and computer equipment. This creates a material, non-cash tailwind to Operating Income by reducing depreciation expense. This accounting shift suggests management is "sweating" AWS assets longer to offset rising infrastructure CapEx, potentially masking underlying margin compression.
• **SBC Concentration Risk:** Share-based compensation (SBC) expense recognition is heavily front-loaded; >50% of total unrecognized compensation cost is slated for recognition within the next 12 months. Given the 2-year (P2Y) vesting period focus, this indicates a significant GAAP earnings headwind through H1 2026 despite flat headcount.
• **Hidden Operational Shifts:** Substantial unrecorded purchase obligations for "leases not yet commenced" and "long-term agreements to acquire digital media and energy" ($57, $58) signal aggressive capacity scaling in AWS and Prime Video that is not yet reflected on the balance sheet. 
• **Liquidity & Capital Allocation:** Continued reliance on the $10B March 2022 share repurchase program with a pivot toward Commercial Paper for short-term liquidity. Pace of debt refinancing for Senior Notes (2014-2022 tranches) remains a priority as interest expense sensitivity increases.
• **Segment KPI Trajectory:**
  - **AWS:** Shift in RPO (Remaining Performance Obligation) duration; focus on energy procurement contracts ($58) suggests power availability is now a primary bottleneck for ARR growth.
  - **Retail:** High-margin 3P Seller Services and Advertising Services continue to decouple from Online Stores GMV, indicating a structural shift to a "toll-booth" model with lower inventory risk.
• **Regulatory/Macro Risk:** Materially modified risk language regarding "technology equipment" useful lives suggests a vulnerability to rapid hardware obsolescence (AI chip cycles) that could force future write-downs if the Jan 1 extension proves over-optimistic.
