• **Asset Rationalization:** Executed divestiture of South Africa fiber operations on 03/06/2025. This signals a definitive pivot away from high-CapEx, non-core international fiber towards high-margin tower and data center core assets.
• **Debt Maturity Wall:** Significant refinancing risk concentrated in 2025/2026. Multiple Senior Notes (including 2.950%, 2.400%, 1.375%, and 4.000% tranches) mature in 2025. Refinancing these sub-3% coupons at prevailing 2025 market rates represents a material headwind to AFFO growth per share.
• **Data Center Structural Leakage:** The Stonepeak Partners LP interest in the Data Center segment includes a preferred stock layer. This creates a priority return hurdle that may cap common equity upside from CoreSite’s organic growth in the mid-term.
• **Service Revenue Signal:** Disaggregation of revenue shows "Services Revenue" (installation/cabling) as a potential leading indicator of carrier CapEx deceleration. Any contraction here historically precedes a slowdown in "Property Revenue" organic growth by 2–3 quarters.
• **Intangible Drag:** Amortization of "Acquired Network Locations" and "Customer Relationships" is pegged to a maximum 20-year life. A significant portion of the $114M+ in recent intangibles is tied to legacy European/LatAm acquisitions, potentially masking underlying cash flow strength but weighing on GAAP EPS.
• **Liquidity Profile:** Reliance on the 2021 Multicurrency Credit Facility and 2021 Term Loan remains high. Floating rate exposure on these facilities (typically SOFR-based) creates immediate sensitivity to Fed policy pivots.
• **Segment KPI:** Data Center "Non-Lease Property Revenue" (power/cooling pass-throughs) is increasingly volatile. While technically revenue-neutral, it inflates top-line growth figures without contributing to EBITDA margin expansion.
