• **GUIDANCE DOWNGRADE:** FY2022 reported diluted EPS revised to $5.39–$5.50 (vs. $6.12–$6.30 initial Feb guidance), a ~12% cut directly attributed to Russia deconsolidation and $0.03/share impact from Ukraine operations. Pro forma organic revenue growth remains at 4%–6% despite volume headwinds.
• **RUSSIA CONCENTRATION RISK:** Russia represented ~10% of 2021 total shipment volume and 6.3% of Q1 2022 net revenues ($499M). Management recorded $210M in pre-tax charges for asset impairment and inventory write-downs following decision to exit the market.
• **HTU MOMENTUM VS. CIGARETTE EROSION:** Heated Tobacco Unit (HTU) volume grew 14.2% to 24.8B units, while combustible cigarette volume fell 0.6% to 170.1B units. IQOS users reached 17.9M (excluding Russia/Ukraine), adding ~1M users in Q1 despite device supply constraints.
• **OPERATIONAL FRICTION:** Semiconductor shortages identified as the primary bottleneck for IQOS ILUMA expansion. Management signaled "intentional HTU inventory buffering" in the EU to mitigate supply chain volatility, an atypical working capital shift.
• **SEGMENT PERFORMANCE:** EU HTU market share gained 1.3 points to 6.0%; East Asia/Australia HTU share climbed 3.0 points to 13.0%, driven by Japan market penetration.
• **CAPITAL ALLOCATION:** Issued €1.25B in debt (March 2022) across two tranches (2.875% and 3.375% coupons) to bolster liquidity. No share buybacks executed in Q1; dividend payments of $1.9B remains the priority.
• **R&D PIVOT:** Wellness and Healthcare segment (Vectura/Fertin) reported $18M operating loss on $57M revenue, highlighting high-burn phase of non-tobacco transition.
