• **Aggressive Debt Maturity Extension:** MPC Parent issued Senior Notes (due 2030, 2035) on Feb 10, 2025, and MPLX subsidiary issued long-dated Senior Notes (due 2035, 2055) on Mar 10, 2025. This pivot to 30-year paper signals management’s intent to lock in long-term capital structures and term out maturing liabilities (specifically May/June 2025 maturities) ahead of potential macro-volatility.

• **Inorganic Midstream Acceleration:** The Whiptail Midstream acquisition (closed Mar 11, 2025) represents a material shift in capital allocation toward gathering and processing. This follows the Mar 2024 Ohio Gathering/Condensate deal, confirming a trend of aggressive consolidation in the Midstream segment to offset Refining cyclicality.

• **Feedstock Margin Sensitivity:** High-volume derivative layering in soybean oil and blending products identifies a heightened operational focus on hedging Renewable Diesel input costs. The long/short mix suggests tactical positioning against feedstock price compression, a critical risk to the Renewable Diesel segment's profitability.

• **Liquidity Fortification:** Maintenance of the MPC Revolving Credit Facility (due July 2027) and Trade Receivables Securitization (due Sept 2027) supports the pace of share repurchases under the Nov 2024 authorization. This structural liquidity ensures buyback continuity despite R&M margin fluctuations.

• **Segment KPI - Midstream Growth:** Acquisition activity and increased throughput in the Delaware Basin (via Whiptail) are now the primary drivers for MPLX, as opposed to organic maintenance capex.

• **Risk Modification:** Refinancing of MarkWest Senior Notes (due June 2025) as a subsequent event (Apr 9, 2025) removes a key short-term credit overhang.
