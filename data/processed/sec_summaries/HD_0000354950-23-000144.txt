* **Guidance Downward Pivot:** Material negative revision to FY2023 outlook; Net Sales and Comp Sales now projected to decrease 2% to 5% (previously "flat"). Operating margin floor lowered to 14.0% (from 14.5%); Diluted EPS now expected to contract 7% to 13% YoY.
* **Volume-Traffic Divergence:** Average ticket grew nominally (0.2%) to $90.03, but Comp Transactions collapsed by 4.8% YoY. Big-ticket transactions (>$1,000) decreased 6.5%, signaling a non-obvious acceleration in consumer deferral of discretionary, high-margin projects.
* **Deflationary Impact:** Management identified a $1.0B (approx. 270 bps) headwind from lumber price deflation alone. Negative sentiment is currently attributed to "extreme weather" in California/West, yet raw data indicates broad-based geographic traffic erosion.
* **Inventory Risk:** Inventory sits at $25.4B. Despite lumber deflation, inventory value remains high relative to the 4.2% total sales decline, suggesting a build-up of non-commodity goods and potential markdown risk in late-season categories.
* **KPI Trajectory:** Total Comp Sales fell 4.5% (US Comps -4.6%). This marks a significant deceleration from the +0.5% reported in Q4 2022, indicating the "normalization" phase is overshooting to the downside.
* **Capital Allocation:** Repurchased $2.1B of shares in Q1; shares outstanding reduced to 1,005M (as of May 16) from 1,021M YoY. Cash dividend commitment remains high at $2.09/share ($8.4B annualized), potentially constraining R&D or expansion capex if operating margins compress toward the 14% floor.
* **Modified Risks:** Management transitioned focus from "supply chain constraints" to "macroeconomic volatility" and "mortgage lock-in" effects as primary threats to the "Pro" customer backlog and home equity-driven demand.
