• **FY25 Guidance Withdrawal:** Full-year outlook retracted; Investor Day postponed indefinitely. Signals a total strategic reset under incoming CEO Elliott Hill (effective Oct 14), shifting from "Direct-led" back to wholesale-integrated.
• **DTC Strategy Failure:** NIKE Direct revenue $4.7B (-13% YoY) vs. Wholesale $6.4B (-8% YoY). Accelerated decline in digital (-15%) confirms the loss of the "Direct-first" premium and necessitates a pivot back to retail partners to recapture traffic.
• **Margin-Revenue Divergence:** Gross Margin expanded 120 bps to 45.4% despite a 10% revenue collapse ($11.59B). Alpha signal: Margin strength is non-structural, driven by the $2B "Save to Invest" restructuring and lower logistics/freight, rather than product pricing power.
• **Inventory Purge:** $8.3B (-5% YoY). Management is aggressively liquidating "Lifestyle" franchises (Dunk, Air Force 1, Jordan 1) to reduce overexposure; unit counts are down double-digits, indicating a massive SKU rationalization cycle.
• **Segment KPI Decay:** North America Footwear -14% ($3.5B); Converse -15% ($504M). Growth in "Performance Running" is insufficient to offset the rapid contraction in core lifestyle franchises.
• **Greater China Sensitivity:** $1.7B (-4% reported). Management tone shifted from "resilient" to "volatile," specifically citing declining digital traffic and intensified promotional pressure in the region.
• **Capital Allocation Floor:** Repurchased 14.2M shares for $1.2B in Q1 (avg. price ~$84.50). Continued aggressive buyback pace despite top-line contraction suggests an attempt to support valuation during a period of zero visibility.
• **Atypical Risk:** Increased emphasis on "speed-to-market" as a competitive vulnerability against agile incumbents (On, Hoka), signaling Nike's lost dominance in the technical performance cycle.
