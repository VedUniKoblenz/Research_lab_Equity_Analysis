• **Yield-Volume Divergence**: US Domestic ADV grew 6.5% YoY—the first positive inflection in nine quarters—but Revenue per Piece (RPP) fell 2.2% to $15.65. This confirms a bearish mix-shift toward lower-margin Ground services and aggressive SMB incentive pricing that management’s "return to growth" narrative partially obscures.

• **Strategic Asset Pivot**: Finalized $1.025B sale of Coyote Logistics (SCS segment) on 09/16/2024. Shift from volatile, low-margin truckload brokerage to high-barrier healthcare logistics is accelerating; SCS adjusted operating margin reached 8.5% despite the revenue headwind from the divestiture.

• **Operational Cost Step-Up**: Year 2 of the Teamsters contract triggered a $0.75/hour wage increase effective 08/01/2024. Management "expects" productivity to offset, but US Domestic operating margin remains compressed at 6.7% (GAAP) vs. historical double-digits, signaling a higher permanent floor for labor COGS.

• **Guidance Tightening**: Revised FY24 revenue guidance downward to $91.1B (from previous $92.0B–$94.5B range). Consolidated Adj. Operating Margin target set at 9.4%, requiring a significant Q4 margin expansion to hit.

• **Capital Allocation Signals**: Resumed share repurchases with $500M executed in Q3; FY24 target set at $1.0B. Issued $1.0B 4.450% Senior Notes (2030) and $750M 4.850% Senior Notes (2054) to refinance near-term maturities, locking in higher cost of debt.

• **Segment KPI Trajectory**:
  - International: Export volume -0.6% YoY, but currency-neutral RPP +2.5% indicates durable pricing power in premium cross-border lanes.
  - SCS: Revenue +8.0% YoY, driven by ocean/air freight recovery, despite the Coyote divestiture impact.
