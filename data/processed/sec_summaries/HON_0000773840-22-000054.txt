• **Sentiment Divergence:** Management maintains a bullish outlook on "Aerospace recovery," yet raw financial data shows Commercial OE (Original Equipment) remains severely constrained by supply chain friction. Margin expansion is currently reliant on "Price-Over-Volume" (POV) rather than unit throughput, creating a risk if price elasticity shifts in H2.

• **Hidden Operational Shifts:**
  - **Inventory Build:** Inventory surged to **$5.52B** (up from $4.95B at FYE21). This 11.5% increase reflects "safety stock" accumulation, tying up working capital to hedge against volatile semiconductor and raw material lead times.
  - **Accounts Receivable:** AR rose to **$8.1B**, outpacing sales growth velocity, suggesting potential extension of credit terms to support distributors in HBT and SPS.
  - **M&A Pivot:** Integration of **US Digital Design, Inc.** (closed Jan 18, 2022) for **$158M** signals a strategic R&D pivot toward high-margin, software-defined "First Responder" tech within the HBT segment.

• **Segment-Specific KPI Trajectory:**
  - **Aerospace:** Backlog reached **$29.5B** (+12% YoY). The critical alpha signal is the 20% surge in Commercial Aftermarket, which is subsidizing the 10% decline in Defense & Space volumes.
  - **SPS (Safety and Productivity Solutions):** Warehouse & Workflow (Intelligrated) orders are "moderating" following the COVID-era e-commerce peak. Alpha extraction: Watch the **$2.5B+** RPO in Intelligrated; a decline here precedes a multi-quarter revenue drag.
  - **PMT (Performance Materials):** UOP orders showed a lumpy but material recovery in catalysts, suggesting a late-cycle refinery CAPEX rebound.
