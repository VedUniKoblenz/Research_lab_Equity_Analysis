• FY23 Net Income guidance raised to $8.75B–$9.25B (prior $8.0B–$8.5B), signaling high confidence ("we expect") in sustained price realization despite volume volatility. 
• Inventory surged to $10.15B (+28% YoY from $7.9B), a massive build-up that contradicts management's bullishness; high Work-in-Process (WIP) levels suggest significant delivery risk if supply chain bottlenecks do not resolve in H2.
• Production & Precision Ag (PPA) operating margin expanded 890 bps to 23.4%, driven by +13% price realization, offsetting 14% higher production costs. 
• Financial Services provision for credit losses jumped to $38M vs. $3M YoY, a 1,166% increase signaling internal expectations of a turn in the credit cycle and weakening borrower profiles.
• RPO (Remaining Performance Obligations) analysis indicates backlog extends through FY23, yet SAT (Small Ag & Turf) retail sales turned negative in certain categories, suggesting a peak in the small-tractor cycle.
• Share repurchases totaled $1.1B in Q1, maintaining an aggressive pace relative to $2.5B in FCF, prioritizing equity reduction over debt paydown despite rising interest expense.
• Restricted cash includes $44M specifically tied to Russia/Ukraine exposure, reflecting ongoing asset impairment risks not yet fully realized in the P&L.
• R&D spending pivoted toward "Precision Ag" integration; software-actuated features are now the primary driver of ASP (Average Selling Price) increases across Large Ag.
