• **Intangible Obsolescence Risk:** Acquired "Developed Technology" useful life is set at a notably short 4-year weighted average. This implies a high-velocity amortization drag and an accelerated R&D reinvestment requirement to prevent technical debt in the Cerner/Cloud portfolio.
• **RPO Recognition Velocity:** Remaining Performance Obligations (RPO) show a specific timing shift; management indicates a 2-year satisfaction window (P2Y) for a material segment of the backlog, suggesting a 24-month revenue floor but limited near-term acceleration surprise.
• **Interest Rate Exposure:** Despite massive fixed-rate issuance, ORCL maintains active SOFR-based interest rate swaps and term loan facilities. The $4.4B Term Loan One and $1.3B Term Loan Two are tied to SOFR + 0.50% to 1.125%, creating direct sensitivity to FOMC terminal rate revisions.
• **Segment KPI Trajectory:** Cloud Services and License Support (recurring) now carry a 7-year useful life for related customer relationships, contrasting with the 4-year tech life, signaling a strategy of "locking in" legacy bases while aggressively cycling through underlying software iterations.
