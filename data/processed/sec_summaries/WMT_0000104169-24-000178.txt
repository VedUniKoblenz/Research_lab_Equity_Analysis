• **Inventory Velocity & Decoupling:** Global inventory remains flat YoY despite a 5.5% increase in net sales; sales growth is now structurally decoupled from inventory investment, signaling a permanent shift in AI-driven replenishment efficiency and reduced markdown risk for the holiday quarter.
• **High-Income Cohort Capture:** Walmart U.S. comp sales (+5.3%) and transaction growth (+3.1%) were disproportionately driven by households earning >$100k/year, indicating a successful trade-down capture that management "expects" to retain through digital convenience.
• **Margin Mix Shift:** Global advertising revenue (Walmart Connect) grew 28% YoY, while E-commerce grew 27%. These high-margin "Value Added Services" are now the primary driver of the 8.2% adjusted operating income growth, which significantly outpaced top-line growth.
• **Guidance Upward Revision:** Management moved from "anticipate" to "expect" regarding raised FY25 targets: Net Sales growth 4.8%–5.1% (up from 3.75%–4.75%) and Adjusted EPS of $2.42–$2.47.
• **JD.com Exit & Liquidity:** Fully divested the remaining JD.com equity stake in Q3, generating $3.6B in cash proceeds. This pivot signals a retreat from passive international equity in favor of direct investment in proprietary fulfillment and the Sam’s Club China expansion.
• **Capital Allocation:** Repurchased $1.0B in shares during Q3 ($3.1B YTD). $13.4B remains under current authorization.
• **Segment KPI Trajectory:** Sam’s Club membership income grew 15.0%, hitting a record high, while Walmart U.S. delivery volume surged 50%+, highlighting the transition from a brick-and-mortar retailer to a logistics-first platform.
