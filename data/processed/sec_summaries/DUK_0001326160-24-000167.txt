**Senior Equity Research Analyst: DUK Q2 2024 Quantitative Alpha Summary**

*   **Sentiment Divergence:** Management remains aggressive on a "30,000 MW economic development pipeline" (primarily AI/Data Centers), yet raw financial data shows a significant drag from interest expenses ($1.7B+ for H1 2024) and regulatory lag. Bullishness on "unprecedented" load growth contradicts the actual H1 residential volume weakness, which showed sensitivity to rate hikes and price elasticity despite favorable weather-normalized adjustments.
*   **Hidden Operational Shifts:** Following the full divestiture of the Commercial Renewables segment, DUK has pivoted entirely to a 100% regulated model. Significant headcount optimization and "agile" restructuring are being utilized to mitigate O&M pressure. Atypical trend: Rising accounts receivable aging in the Florida segment suggests localized consumer credit stress.
*   **Forward-Looking Nuance:** Management "expects" (high confidence) a $73B 5-year capital plan through 2028. Reaffirmed 2024 EPS guidance range of $5.85–$6.10. Technical shift: Transitioned from "anticipating" to "executing" on the North Carolina Multi-Year Rate Plan (MYRP), providing high visibility into 2025-2026 revenue requirements.
*   **Atypical Risk Factors:** **MODIFIED:** Technology execution risk regarding the integration of Small Modular Reactors (SMRs) into the Carolinas' long-term resource plan. **NEW:** Increased litigation risk surrounding the NC Carbon Plan and potential stranded asset risk for remaining coal capacity if environmental mandates accelerate beyond current depreciation schedules. 
*   **Segment-Specific KPIs:** 
    *   **Electric Utilities:** Weather-normalized retail sales growth (forecast +1.5-2.0%); Industrial load growth (lead metric for Carolinas segment).
    *   **Gas Utilities:** Customer growth CAGR (1.5%); Piedmont segment NRR (Net Revenue Retention) remaining stable despite volatile gas pricing.
*   **Capital Allocation Signals:** Dividend increased to $1.045/share (July announcement, post-period signal). Debt issuance remains high with $1.1B in DEP and DEF senior notes to fund grid hardening. Notably, management explicitly stated no plans for common equity issuance in 2024, signaling a reliance on internal cash flow and parent-level debt to maintain the 5-7% EPS CAGR.
