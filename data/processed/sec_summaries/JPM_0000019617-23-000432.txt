• **FRB Distortions & Integration:** May 1st acquisition of First Republic Bank (FRB) added $203B in assets and $92B in deposits. This created a +$2.4B impact on non-interest expenses in Q2. Full-year 2023 expense guidance raised to $84.5B, signaling higher-than-expected integration costs and persistent wage inflation.
• **NII Trajectory Divergence:** Management raised 2023 Net Interest Income (NII) guidance to ~$87B (excluding CIB Markets), yet signaled a "peak NII" inflection point. Divergence noted between record Q2 NII and "speculative" deposit beta assumptions; management anticipates accelerated deposit migration to higher-yield products as a primary margin headwind for H2.
• **Credit Reserve Pivot:** Provision for credit losses reached $2.9B. Critical shift in reserve builds: $1.5B net build primarily concentrated in Commercial Real Estate (CRE) within CB and credit card seasoning in CCB. This indicates a proactive, non-standard defensive positioning against office-sector valuations.
• **Segment KPIs (Financials):** Consumer & Community Banking (CCB) NIM expanded to 3.00%+. Average deposits declined 7% YoY, but Card outstandings surged 18% YoY. Corporate & Investment Bank (CIB) IB fees dropped 6% YoY, offset by 10% growth in Payments revenue.
• **Capital Allocation & Regulatory Risk:** CET1 ratio climbed to 13.8% (vs. 11.4% regulatory minimum), providing a $47B buffer. Despite $2B in Q2 buybacks, management tone shifted to "cautious" regarding future pace, citing the Basel III Endgame proposal and an expected ~25% RWA (Risk-Weighted Asset) increase.
• **Hidden Operational Shift:** Headcount increased 8% YoY to 300,000+, driven by FRB onboarding and a pivot toward AI/technology R&D spending, which is now a structural floor in the expense base rather than a variable cost.
