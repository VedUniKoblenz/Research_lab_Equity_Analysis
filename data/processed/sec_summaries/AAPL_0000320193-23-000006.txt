• Strategic Debt Laddering: Apple continues to leverage ultra-low-cost legacy debt, specifically the 0.000% Notes due 2025 and 0.875% Notes due 2025. This positioning provides a substantial interest-rate arbitrage opportunity as the company earns higher yields on its cash equivalents compared to its fixed-rate cost of capital.
• Conversion Risk: The heavy 63% weighting of current RPO makes the company disproportionately sensitive to immediate-term supply chain volatility. Any disruption in fulfillment (similar to the Zhengzhou lockdowns mentioned in the report) translates directly to missed quarterly targets rather than being absorbed by a long-dated backlog.
• Capital Allocation: Common stock par value remains at $0.00001 with active registration of 1.375% Notes due 2024, suggesting a continued strategy of using cheap debt to fund aggressive share buybacks, maintaining EPS growth despite the 2023 revenue contraction.
