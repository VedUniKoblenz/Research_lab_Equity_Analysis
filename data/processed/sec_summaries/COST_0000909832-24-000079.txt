• **Sentiment Divergence:** Management maintains a bullish posture on "member value," yet SG&A deleveraged by 13bps YoY (9.41% of total revenue vs. 9.27% in Q1-24). Despite a 7.7% surge in membership fee revenue ($1,166M) following the Sep 2024 fee hike, the operational tailwind was partially offset by elevated stock-based compensation and rising wage scales, signaling that the "membership annuity" is increasingly subsidizing internal cost inflation rather than pure margin expansion.

• **Hidden Operational Shifts:** Merchandise costs improved as a percentage of net sales to 88.72% (vs. 88.96% YoY). This 24bps expansion in gross margin suggests a shift toward higher-margin private label (Kirkland Signature) penetration or more aggressive vendor volume rebates, effectively hedging against the SG&A creep.

• **Forward-Looking Nuance:** Filings emphasize management "believes" current liquidity ($10B+ in cash/equivalents) is sufficient for the 2025 expansion plan. Use of "believe" regarding global renewal rates (90.5%) vs. "expect" in historical cycles indicates a subtle pivot to defensive monitoring as the Sep 2024 fee increase cycles through the full member base.

• **Capital Allocation:** Total cash returned via dividends reached $514M ($1.16/share). The absence of significant accelerated share repurchases this quarter, despite record net income of $1.75B, suggests a pivot toward preserving dry powder for lumpy CAPEX requirements related to logistics/supply-chain vertical integration.
