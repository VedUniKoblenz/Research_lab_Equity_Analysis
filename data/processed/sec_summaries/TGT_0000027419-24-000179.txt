• **Inventory/Sales Divergence:** Inventory levels climbed to $15.18B (+3.1% YoY), materially outpacing quarterly sales growth of 1.1% ($25.67B). This suggests a buildup in discretionary stock or defensive over-stocking that threatens future gross margins via markdowns.
• **Margin Contraction Signal:** Net earnings fell 12.1% YoY to $854M. Operating pressure is driven by SG&A deleverage ($5.50B, +3.2% YoY), signaling that labor and digital fulfillment costs are rising faster than the top line.
• **Segment KPI Trajectory:** Growth is dangerously concentrated in Beauty (+5.9% YoY to $4.34B) and Essentials (+3.4% to $6.40B). Conversely, high-margin discretionary segments—Hardlines (-3.8%) and Home (-0.5%)—remain in contraction, indicating a "low-quality" revenue mix shift toward consumables.
• **Capital Allocation Pivot:** Management aggressively resumed share repurchases, deploying $354M in Q3 after a prolonged period of inactivity. This signal suggests a tactical attempt to support EPS despite weakening operational fundamentals.
• **Liquidity/Debt Shift:** TGT issued $1.0B in 4.5% fixed-rate notes maturing 2034. Reliance on short-term funding escalated sharply, with Commercial Paper balances hitting $1.62B vs. $0.00 in the prior-year period, indicating tighter working capital management.
• **Other Revenue Hedge:** "Other Revenue" (Retail Media/Roundel and Credit) grew 11.1% to $671M, acting as the primary buffer against core retail EBIT erosion. Any slowdown in high-margin ad-spend represents a major unpriced risk.
