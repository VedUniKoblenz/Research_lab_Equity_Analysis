• **F-35 TR-3 Delivery Pivot:** 2023 delivery guidance slashed ~27% to 100–120 units (from 147–153). Signal: Hardware production is decoupling from software validation. Management "expects" first TR-3 delivery in Q3/Q4, but "anticipates" software stability, indicating speculative risk of a 2024 spillover.
• **Inventory/Cash Flow Divergence:** Net Cash from Ops fell to $1.5B (vs $2.0B Q2’22). Aeronautics contract assets surged by >$600M, reflecting "parked" F-35 inventory. Signal: Working capital drag will likely persist until TR-3 acceptance, creating a potential $0.5B–$1.0B liquidity bottleneck.
• **Space Segment Margin Compression:** Operating profit down 14% YoY; margins contracted to 9.2% (vs 11.2% Q2’22). Primary driver: ULA equity earnings collapsed to $13M (vs $80M YoY). Signal: Space top-line growth (up 12%) is currently "hollow" due to launch timing and equity investment volatility.
• **Backlog Technicals:** Record backlog of $158.01B (+5.3% YTD). Signal: Book-to-bill remains >1.0, but realization is slowing in MFC (tactical missiles down 8% on volume).
• **Guidance Conflict:** Management raised FY23 Sales guidance to $66.25B–$66.75B (+1.9%) despite the F-35 delivery halt. Signal: Aggressive revenue recognition via percentage-of-completion accounting is masking physical delivery delays.
• **Capital Allocation:** Repurchased 3.1M shares for $1.5B in Q2; $6.0B remains on authorization. Pace is steady despite a 25% YoY drop in free cash flow.
• **MFC Segment Risk:** Operating profit down 4% on lower production of tactical missiles. Signal: HIMARS/GMLRS ramp-up is not yet offsetting the margin tail-off in mature programs.
