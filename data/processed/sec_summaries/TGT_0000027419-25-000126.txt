* **Debt Refinancing & WACC Pressure**: TGT is aggressively terming out low-cost debt. The filing confirms the retirement of 2.25% 2025 notes, replaced by significantly higher-cost capital, including 5.0% maturing 2035 and 5.25% maturing 2036. This 275-300 bps spread on new issuance signals a structural headwind to interest expense and net margins through 2026.
* **Non-Core Revenue Decoupling**: Roundel (Advertising Revenue) and Credit Card Profit Sharing are increasingly vital to margin stability. These high-margin streams are being tracked with higher granularity, indicating management's reliance on "other revenue" to offset core merchandising margin compression.
* **Settlement Tailwinds**: The Credit Card Interchange Fee Litigation reached a settlement stage in H1 2025. The Q3 filing reflects the finalization of these impacts, providing a non-recurring boost to operating cash flow that masks underlying traffic-driven pressure.
* **Inventory & Supply Chain Pivot**: Transition from "We believe" to "We expect" regarding supply chain stabilization. Significant focus on "Supply Chain and Digital Fulfillment Costs" (Metric 168-171) indicates a shift in Capex toward back-end automation rather than store-front expansion. 
* **KPI Trajectory**: 
    - Retail: Same-store sales (SSS) remains the primary signal, but Average Order Value (AOV) is showing sensitivity to the "Household Essentials" vs "Hardlines" mix shift.
    - Digital: Fulfillment cost per transaction is the "hidden" KPI; management signals these costs are being mitigated by "Sortation Centers," though the benefit is partially offset by higher commercial paper rates (Context 143).
* **Capital Allocation**: 452.8M shares outstanding as of Nov 19, 2025. Buyback pace remains opportunistic rather than programmatic, prioritized behind the 2025-2026 debt maturity ladder.
* **Operational Restructuring**: Shift in "Merchandising" costs (Context 164) suggests a headcount pivot toward digital proprietary brands, deprecating traditional legacy categories.
