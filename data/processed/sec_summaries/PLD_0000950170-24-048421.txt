• **Guidance Retraction:** Lowered FY2024 Core FFO guidance to $5.37–$5.47 per share (from $5.42–$5.56), signaling a ~1.2% midpoint haircut. This contradicts historical Q1 patterns of maintaining or raising guidance, highlighting a sharp pivot in macro-expectations.
• **Sentiment Divergence:** Despite record-level lease spreads (67.6% net effective), management lowered Same Store Cash NOI growth projections to 6.25%–7.25% (previously 8.0%–9.0%). This reflects a "valuation vs. velocity" trap: high paper rents are being neutralized by increasing downtime and tenant "decision paralysis."
• **Operational Retrenchment:** Slashed FY2024 development starts by $500M (midpoint $2.75B vs. prior $3.25B). The decrease in "build-to-suit" activity suggests a cooling R&D/CapEx cycle among major e-commerce and 3PL tenants.
• **Regional Weakness:** Specific operational drag identified in Southern California and the Inland Empire; these high-barrier markets are seeing uncharacteristic inventory digestion, leading to a downward revision in average occupancy to 96.0%–97.0%.
• **Strategic Capital Pivot:** Reduced expected 2024 Strategic Capital income to $530M–$550M (down from $550M–$600M), signaling a slowdown in promote timing and fee-generation from co-investment ventures (USLV, EUF, JRF).
• **Capital Allocation:** Issued $1.1B in senior notes in Q1 (including 30-year USD at 5.250% and 5-year CAD at 4.875%). Rising cost of debt is narrowing the spread against stabilized cap rates, currently pressured by a higher-for-longer rate environment.
• **KPI Deterioration:** Retention fell to 74.3% (vs. 77.2% in Q4 '23). Net absorption across the portfolio is tracking 25% below 10-year averages, forcing a shift from "rent maximization" to "occupancy protection."
