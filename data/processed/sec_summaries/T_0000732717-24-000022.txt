• **FCF Acceleration**: Q1 Free Cash Flow reached $3.1B, a significant 210% increase YoY from $1.0B, signaling a decoupling from historical Q1 seasonality. Direct path to $17B–$18B FY24 guidance, supported by lower capex intensity of $3.8B ($4.6B total investment).
• **KPI Divergence (Mobility)**: Postpaid phone ARPU rose to $56.68 (+0.9% YoY) despite a 5.5% decline in equipment revenue ($4.8B). Signal: Growth is driven by plan migration and pricing power rather than hardware cycles, which management "expects" to persist despite macroeconomic tightening.
• **Fiber Growth Velocity**: AT&T Fiber added 252k subscribers, maintaining a >200k run-rate for 17 quarters. Fiber ARPU of $68.61 (+4.2% YoY) is the primary hedge against the Business Wireline collapse. 
• **Business Wireline Erosion**: Segment EBITDA fell 18.2% to $1.3B. Management "anticipates" stabilization, yet legacy voice/data revenue contraction (-11.5%) continues to outpace Fiber-to-the-Business gains, indicating a structural rather than cyclical decline.
• **Lead Indicator (RPO)**: Remaining Performance Obligations (RPO) duration is fixed at 21 months (Weighted Average). This provides a hard floor for service revenue valuation but limits upside from sudden enterprise spending re-acceleration.
• **Operational Pivot**: Restructuring signals hidden in $322M of "Other" expenses, largely tied to the 5G Open RAN transition and the $2B+ cost savings initiative tracking toward 2026.
• **Debt/Liquidity**: Retired $2.4B in Global Notes due March 2024. Net Debt-to-Adjusted EBITDA target of 2.5x by 1H25 remains firm, implying a continued pause on share buybacks in favor of deleveraging.
