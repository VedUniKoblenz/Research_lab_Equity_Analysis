• **Sentiment Divergence**: Management raised FY22 organic sales guidance to 4–5% (prior 2–4%) and buyback targets to $9–$10B despite a massive 400 bps headwind to Gross Margin from commodity/freight costs. • **Hidden Operational Shifts**: Inventory surged 14.4% YTD to $6.84B, far outpacing sales growth, signaling defensive stockpiling against supply chain volatility. Accounts Receivable rose 10.1% YTD to $5.21B, suggesting potential loosening of credit terms to drive volume.

  - **Beauty**: Skin/Personal organic sales declined low-single digits due to SK-II brand weakness in China travel retail.
  - **Fabric/Home**: Organic sales +8%; Home Care declined low-single digits vs. high COVID base.

• **Forward-Looking Nuance**: Management "expects" (high confidence) $2.8B in after-tax headwinds ($2.3B commodities, $300M freight, $200M FX), a $500M escalation from Q1 estimates. Price hikes are lagging cost inputs, with pricing only contributing 3% to growth vs. 4% volume.

• **Capital Allocation**: Aggressive acceleration of buybacks. Q2 repurchases hit $4B; FY22 guidance raised by $1B–$2B. Dividend payout "expected" at ~$8B. 

• **Atypical Risk**: Gillette Indefinite-lived intangible ($14.4B) cushion is thinning. Fair value exceeds carrying value by only 16%; a 25 bps increase in discount rate would reduce excess fair value by $1.1B. Significant exposure to China travel retail policy shifts.
