• **Macro-Provisioning Buffer:** Management has pegged its "Current Economic Outlook" unemployment rate at 4.5% for CECL modeling (XBRL: `bac:UnemploymentRateCurrentEconomicOutlook`). This represents a ~40bps conservative overlay compared to the actual 4.1% period-end rate, suggesting a hidden "rainy day" reserve that could be released to boost EPS if the labor market remains resilient.
• **Duration Hedging Pivot:** Significant focus on hedging Long-Term Debt (XBRL: `f-1177`) indicates a shift toward protecting Net Interest Income (NII) against a potential "lower-for-longer" rate scenario, diverging from the previous "higher-for-longer" defensive stance.
• **NII Trajectory:** Management signals "high confidence" that Q2 2024 represents the NII trough. Expectation for sequential growth in Q3 and Q4 is underpinned by asset repricing and a deceleration in deposit beta migration to high-yield products.
• **Tactical Credit Management:** Short-term financing receivable modifications are being capped at a 3-month duration (XBRL: `bac:FinancingReceivableModifiedTerm`), indicating a preference for tactical "bridge" relief over structural loan restructuring in the consumer segment.
• **Regulatory Capital Drag:** Tier 1 Common Equity (CET1) ratio at 11.9% remains 120bps above current requirements, but the "Basel III Endgame" revisions provide a material headwind to future share buyback acceleration.
• **Consumer Health KPIs:** Despite inflationary pressure, average card spend increased 3% YoY, though Net Charge-offs (NCOs) in the credit card segment have normalized to pre-pandemic levels (3.88%), removing the credit-quality "alpha" enjoyed in prior years.
• **Expense Efficiency:** Restructuring and headcount attrition (down ~5,000 YoY) are being offset by a pivot to $3.8B/year R&D and tech investment, specifically targeting AI-driven wealth management tools in the GWIM segment.
