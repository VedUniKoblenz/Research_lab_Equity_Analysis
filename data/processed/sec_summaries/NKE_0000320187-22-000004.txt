• **Greater China Contagion:** Revenue collapsed 20% YoY ($1,844M) and 24% on a currency-neutral basis. Significant sentiment divergence: management cites "brand strength" while raw EBIT in the region plummeted 36% YoY, driven by inventory liquidation and heavy promotional activity to offset factory closures in Vietnam.

• **In-Transit Inventory Bloat:** Total inventory $6,509M (+7% YoY). Internal data shows "in-transit" composition hit record highs as transit lead times spiked 80–100% vs. pre-pandemic levels (8 weeks delay in NA). This creates a "phantom inventory" risk where seasonal product arrives late, necessitating future gross margin-dilutive markdowns.

• **Digital Deceleration:** NIKE Direct grew 9% ($4.7B), but NIKE Digital revenue grew only 12% (11% currency-neutral). This represents a material sequential cooling from the 50-80% growth rates seen in FY21, suggesting a "reversion to mean" for the high-margin digital segment.

• **Gross Margin Anomaly:** GM expanded 280 bps to 45.9% despite $100M+ in elevated freight costs. This was sustained by "full-price realization" (fewer discounts), a trend vulnerable to the aforementioned 8-week delivery lag and stale seasonal stock.

• **Aggressive Capital Defense:** Buybacks accelerated to $1.2B (7.3M shares) at an average cost of ~$164.38/share, a 33% increase in pace vs. Q1 FY22. Dividend payout increased 11% ($484M), signaling a tactical pivot to support share price amid China volatility.

• **Supply Chain Concentration:** 51% of footwear and 30% of apparel sourced from Vietnam. The "We expect" guidance for logistics recovery is high-confidence but contradicts the "We anticipate" speculative language regarding the full resumption of Vietnam factory capacity and labor availability.
