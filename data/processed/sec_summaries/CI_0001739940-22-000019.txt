• **MCR Improvement Divergence:** Medical Care Ratio (MCR) improved to 80.7% in Q2 2022 vs. 84.4% in Q2 2021. Despite inflationary macro concerns, the 370 bps contraction driven by higher premiums and favorable prior-year development suggests pricing power outstripping medical cost trends.
• **Strategic Portfolio Pivot:** Completed sale of International Life, Accident, and Supplemental Benefits businesses on July 1, 2022, for ~$5.4B cash. This exit from capital-intensive insurance significantly shifts the mix toward the "Evernorth" capital-light fee model.
• **Aggressive Capital Redistribution:** Repurchased 15.6M shares for $3.7B in H1 2022 (including a $3.5B ASR). Remaining repurchase authority of $4.1B as of August 3, 2022, indicates a pace significantly exceeding 2021 levels.
• **Evernorth Scalability:** Segment adjusted earnings reached $1.5B (+7% YoY). Adjusted pharmacy scripts grew to 411M in Q2, indicating NRR stability despite a competitive PBM environment.
• **Cigna Healthcare Customer Concentration:** Total medical customers reached 17.8M. Growth is increasingly reliant on US Government (Medicare Advantage) and International Health segments, as US Commercial remains mature.
• **Liquidity/Refinancing Logic:** Established new credit facilities in April 2022 totaling $5B (including a $3B facility due 2027 and a $1B 364-day facility). High commercial paper reliance ($2.8B outstanding) exposes CI to rising short-term rates, though partially offset by the $5.4B international sale proceeds.
• **Hidden Liability Shift:** Unpaid claims liability stood at $13.6B. A decrease in "Other Comprehensive Loss" to $1.2B (from $2.4B) suggests a significant impact from rising interest rates on the fair value of fixed-income assets, though CI is holding these to maturity to avoid realized losses.
