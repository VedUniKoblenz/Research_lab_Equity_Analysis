**Senior Equity Research Technical Summary: CHTR Q1 2026**

*   **Aggressive Capital Erasure:** Shares outstanding plummeted from 126,631,549 (Dec 31, 2025) to 122,984,536 (Mar 31, 2026). Charter retired ~3.65M shares (~2.9% of the company) in a single quarter, signaling a massive acceleration in buyback intensity and a management view that equity remains fundamentally undervalued relative to cable/mobile convergence cash flows.
*   **Cox Strategic Consolidation:** Filing confirms a major structural transaction ("Cox Contribution" and "Cox Equity Sale"). The issuance of "Convertible Preferred Units" and a new "Common Class C" share class indicates a non-cash, equity-heavy acquisition or joint venture structure with Cox Communications, likely aimed at footprint synergies or a unified mobile/fixed-wireless backbone.
*   **Mobile Financing Pivot:** Emergence of a "Supplier Finance Program" specifically for "Mobile Handsets" (classified under Accounts Payable and Accrued Liabilities) reveals a shift toward leveraging vendor credit to mask the capital intensity of the mobile scale-up. This operational pivot preserves OCF but builds significant current liability pressure.
*   **EIP Credit Expansion:** Significant growth in "Equipment Installment Plan (EIP) Receivables" confirms mobile is transitioning from a "Value-Added Service" to a core hardware-centric segment. This introduces consumer credit risk and working capital drag previously absent from the pure-play cable model.
*   **Legacy FX Risk Concentration:** Technical reliance on Level 2 Currency Swaps to hedge Sterling-denominated Senior Notes (Legacy Time Warner Cable, 5.750% due 2031; 5.250% due 2042). Non-operating income is increasingly sensitive to GBP/USD volatility and swap counterparty risk.
*   **Segment KPI Trajectory:** 
    *   **Mobile:** Technical shift to EIP-driven growth; handset supplier obligations are now a primary current liability.
    *   **Connectivity:** Residential Internet remains the primary defensive anchor, but the "Cox 1.00 Member" designation suggests a looming resegmentation of the enterprise/connectivity business.
*   **Debt Nuance:** Continued reliance on Senior Secured Notes (CCO Holdings) vs. Senior Unsecured Notes; interest rate risk is being mitigated by extending maturities into 2026/2027 via refinancing of $1.5B+ in maturing debt tranches.
