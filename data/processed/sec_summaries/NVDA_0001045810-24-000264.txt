• **Blackwell Yield/Margin Compression:** Management "expects" Blackwell revenue of "several billion dollars" in Q4, but GAAP Gross Margin fell 330bps QoQ to 75.1%. Compression was driven by $908M in inventory provisions and rework costs related to Blackwell low-yield silicon—a direct contradiction to the "seamless transition" narrative.
• **AR vs. Revenue Divergence:** Accounts Receivable surged 42% QoQ to $14.1B, significantly outpacing the 15% QoQ revenue growth. This signals potential pull-forward of demand or aggressive credit terms to Tier-1 CSPs. Four customers now represent 46% of total AR ($6.5B concentration risk).
• **Purchase Commitment Surge:** Total supply chain obligations spiked to $27.8B (from $16.1B YoY). Specifically, inventory purchase obligations rose to $16.7B, and long-term supply/capacity agreements hit $11.1B, signaling a massive, high-stakes bet on H200 and Blackwell production ramp.
• **KPI - RPO Growth:** Remaining Performance Obligations hit $7.47B, a critical lead indicator for software and service-linked recurring revenue. Roughly 43% ($3.2B) is expected to be recognized within the next 12 months.
• **Segment Pivot:** Data Center revenue ($26.3B) now constitutes 87.5% of total top-line, rendering Gaming ($2.9B) and ProViz ($454M) effectively immaterial to valuation.
• **Capital Allocation:** Board approved an additional $50.0B share buyback authorization with no expiration; $15.1B was deployed in H1, indicating an accelerated pace of capital return despite heavy R&D reinvestment ($3.09B in Q2, +52% YoY).
• **Guidance Nuance:** Management "expects" (high confidence) FY25 margins in the "mid-70% range" but "anticipates" (moderate confidence) continued supply constraints, specifically for Blackwell COWoS-L packaging.
