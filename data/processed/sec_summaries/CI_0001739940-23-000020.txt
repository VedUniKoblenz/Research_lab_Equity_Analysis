• **MCR Escalation:** Cigna Healthcare Medical Care Ratio (MCR) expanded to 82.2% in Q2 2023 from 80.7% YoY. Management attributes 150bps deterioration to "increased utilization in Medicare Advantage," specifically outpatient procedures, signaling a shift in the loss-cost trend that contradicts prior-year stability.
• **Evernorth Revenue Concentration:** Segment accounted for $74.5B of $94.4B total H1 2023 revenue (79% concentration). Higher-margin specialty pharmacy services are being utilized to buffer the MCR expansion in the Healthcare segment.
• **Debt Refinancing & Interest Headwinds:** Issued $4.5B in senior notes in Q1/Q2 2023 ($1.25B @ 5.685% due 2026; $2.25B @ 5.40% due 2033; $1.0B @ 5.40% due 2033). These coupons significantly exceed the interest rates on the $2.1B in debt repaid in H1 2023, locking in higher structural interest expense for the next decade.
• **Capital Allocation:** H1 2023 share repurchases totaled $2.3B (8.4M shares). Despite higher debt costs, the pace of buybacks indicates a commitment to EPS accretion over balance sheet deleveraging.
• **Liquidity Overhaul:** Replaced prior credit facilities with a new $4.0B five-year revolving facility (maturing April 2028) and a $1.0B 364-day facility. Total backup liquidity remains at $5.0B, but with revised benchmark rate (SOFR) transition nuances.
• **Regulatory Risk Pivot:** Filing includes NEW emphasis on legislative threats to the Pharmacy Benefit Manager (PBM) model, specifically regarding "transparency requirements" and "340B drug pricing" litigation, posing a tail-risk to Evernorth’s fee-for-service margin.
• **Segment KPI - Cigna Healthcare:** Total medical customers reached 19.5M, but growth is skewed toward lower-margin segments; Medicare Advantage utilization is the primary "Expectation" driver for medical liability adjustments.
• **Forward-Looking Nuance:** Management "expects" medical cost trends to remain elevated for the remainder of 2023, a shift from previous "anticipations" of a return to baseline.
