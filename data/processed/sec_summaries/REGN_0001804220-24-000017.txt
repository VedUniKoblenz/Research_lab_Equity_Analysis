• **Eylea Franchise Transition:** US Eylea net sales dropped to $1.40B (down 14% YoY), only partially offset by $200M from Eylea HD. Total US franchise revenue of $1.60B (down 2% YoY) reveals net erosion despite HD launch, signaling aggressive biosimilar pricing pressure ahead of 2024/2025 competition.

• **Inventory Surge:** Inventories rose to $1.97B (+12% QoQ) from $1.76B at YE23. Build-up suggests management anticipation of rapid Eylea HD global uptake and potential supply chain cushioning for Libtayo expansion.

• **Capital Allocation Escalation:** Board authorized a new $3.0B share buyback program in March 2024, bringing total capacity to ~$4.2B. This follows $423M in Q1 repurchases, indicating a tactical shift to defend valuation as the Eylea patent cliff nears.

• **Concentration Risk:** Dupixent (Sanofi collaboration) profit share reached $1.11B, now accounting for ~35% of total revenue. Increasing reliance on Sanofi’s commercial execution offsets domestic Eylea volatility but narrows the "alpha" to single-asset performance.

• **R&D Pivot:** R&D spend increased 10% YoY to $1.21B, specifically targeting high-confidence pipeline assets in costimulatory bispecifics (oncology) and immunology, while deprecating lower-margin legacy programs.

• **IRA Vulnerability:** New risk language explicitly flags Medicare Part B negotiation impacts under the Inflation Reduction Act. Management "anticipates" (moderate confidence) significant pricing headwinds for the aflibercept franchise starting in 2026, a shift from previous "speculative" labeling.

• **Account Receivable Trends:** Receivables rose to $3.57B (+5% QoQ), slightly outpacing revenue growth, suggesting extended payment terms for providers transitioning to the higher-ASP Eylea HD.
