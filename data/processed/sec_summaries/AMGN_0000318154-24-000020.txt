• **Legacy Erosion Divergence:** Enbrel revenue collapsed 30% YoY to $567M, a severe acceleration of decline vs. historical single-digit erosion, signaling rapid loss of formulary positioning or aggressive competitor rebating not fully offset by volume.
• **Inorganic Growth Dependency:** Q1 revenue growth is entirely inorganic; Horizon assets contributed significantly (TEPEZZA: $424M, KRYSTEXXA: $235M). Excluding these, organic product sales growth remains under pressure as legacy blockbusters like Xgeva (down 2% to $526M) and Aranesp (down 6% to $325M) plateau or shrink.
• **High-Velocity Alpha Drivers:** TEZSPIRE volume/sales surged 80% to $173M, outperforming most analysts' moderate growth projections. Repatha (+33% to $517M) and EVENITY (+38% to $351M) demonstrate sustained momentum in primary care and bone health, providing a critical buffer against biologic erosion.
• **Operational Stability:** Despite the massive Horizon integration, inventory levels remained flat at $5.12B vs. $5.13B at YE23, indicating management has avoided supply chain bloat or "channel stuffing" often seen post-merger.
• **Capital Allocation & Debt:** Long-term debt remains highly concentrated with significant 2025/2026 maturities (e.g., $4.2B Term Loan due April 2025; $2.7B due Oct 2026). Amgen’s ability to deleverage depends on TEPEZZA’s ability to maintain high margins amidst potential rare-disease pricing scrutiny.
• **Investment Exposure:** Equity stake in BeiGene valued at $1.44B and Neumora at $235M; these are mark-to-market volatile assets that could provide non-operating EPS tailwinds/headwinds in upcoming quarters.
• **KPI Trajectory:** BLINCYTO (+33% to $244M) is emerging as a top-tier oncology contributor, suggesting a shift in segment focus toward high-margin, niche biologics.
