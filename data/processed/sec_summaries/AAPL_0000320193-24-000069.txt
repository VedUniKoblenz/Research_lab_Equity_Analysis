• **Capital Allocation Surprise:** Management authorized an unprecedented $110B incremental share buyback (largest in U.S. corporate history), signaling a massive pivot to capital return as top-line hardware growth stalls; dividend increased 4% to $0.25/share.
• **Services Alpha:** Services revenue hit an all-time record of $23.9B (+14% YoY) with a staggering 74.6% gross margin, significantly decoupling from Product gross margins of 36.6%. This reflects a structural shift toward high-margin recurring revenue despite hardware headwinds.
• **Inventory Liquidation:** Total inventory decreased to $5.4B from $6.3B in Sept 2023. This lean positioning suggests aggressive channel clearing ahead of the M4 iPad/AI-PC refresh cycle and mitigates downside risk of component write-downs.
• **China Resilience Divergence:** Greater China revenue declined 8% YoY to $16.4B, yet management’s tone remains disproportionately bullish compared to the double-digit declines seen in earlier Q1/Q2 third-party data, suggesting iPhone 15 Pro Max ASP strength is offsetting local unit volume loss.
• **R&D Intensity:** Quarterly R&D spend rose to $7.9B; 6-month spend reached $15.1B. This reflects a significant reallocation of resources toward GenAI integration and Apple Silicon, even as total net sales contracted 4% YoY.
• **Non-Trade Receivables:** Concentration risk remains high with non-trade receivables at $21.5B; one vendor represents 11% of this balance, highlighting continued supply chain dependency despite diversification rhetoric.
• **RPO Visibility:** Total Remaining Performance Obligations (RPO) of $9.5B (current) provides high-confidence floor for Services revenue over the next 12 months.
