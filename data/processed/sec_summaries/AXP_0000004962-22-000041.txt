• **Credit Pivot Signal:** Total provision for credit losses shifted to a $410M expense compared to a $606M benefit YoY, marking the definitive exhaustion of pandemic-era reserve releases and a return to fundamental credit normalization.
• **Lending vs. Charge Volume:** Cardmember loans surged to $99.5B (+25% YoY), significantly outpacing Cardmember receivables growth ($55.7B). This signals an aggressive operational shift toward revolving interest-bearing balances to capture NII in a rising rate environment, moving away from the traditional low-risk charge model.
• **Margin Compression Disguised by Revenue:** Management "expects" full-year revenue growth of 23-25%, yet GCSG pre-tax income fell 12% to $1.3B. The divergence is driven by a 25% spike in Variable Client Interest (VCI) and reward costs ($3.7B), indicating that acquisition of the Gen Z/Millennial segment (spending +48% YoY) is coming at a significantly higher marginal cost.
• **NII Sensitivity:** Net interest yield on cardmember loans reached 10.7%. With a high concentration of variable-rate assets, the yield expansion acts as a hedge against the 32% increase in total interest expense ($455M), though deposit beta remains a latent risk.
• **Capital Floor Proximity:** Repurchased 8.9M shares in Q2; however, the CET1 ratio declined to 10.3%. With a target range of 10-11%, the pace of buybacks is likely to decelerate in H2 2022 to maintain regulatory buffers.
• **Risk Factor Modification:** Material escalation in macroeconomic sensitivity; management replaced "believes" with "anticipates" regarding credit stability, reflecting moderate confidence in maintaining the 0.8% net write-off rate (principal only) as inflation pressures lower-tier commercial segments.
