• **Guidance Revision (Material Downgrade):** Management lowered FY24 consolidated revenue target to ~$93.0B (previously $92.0B–$94.5B) and slashed adjusted operating margin guidance to 9.4% (previously 10.0%–10.6%). This 60-120 bps margin compression signals structural labor cost absorption issues.

• **Sentiment Divergence:** Management emphasizes a "volume turning point" (U.S. ADV +0.7%—first growth in 9 quarters), yet U.S. Domestic operating profit plummeted 25.8% YoY. Raw data reveals a "hollow recovery" where volume growth is insufficient to offset the 12.1% spike in U.S. labor costs per piece following the Teamsters contract.

• **Operational Pivot (Divestiture):** Definitive agreement to sell Coyote Logistics to RXO for $1.025B. This marks a strategic exit from volatile, low-margin truckload brokerage to reduce cyclicality and capital intensity in the Supply Chain Solutions segment.

• **Cost-Cutting Acceleration:** $1.0B cost-savings target for 2024 via "Fit to Serve" initiative. Headcount reduction focus: 12,000 management positions being eliminated; Q2 results included $100M in restructuring charges.

• **Segment KPIs:**
  - **U.S. Domestic:** ADV +0.7%; Revenue per piece (RPP) -0.3%, indicating a negative product mix shift toward lower-yielding B2C/SurePost.
  - **International:** ADV -2.9%; Operating Margin 16.4% (down from 20.4% YoY), signaling severe operating leverage decay in European markets.

• **Capital Allocation:** Restarted share buybacks in Q2 ($500M); FY24 target set at $1.0B. This signal attempts to provide a price floor despite the guidance cut and 23.3% YoY decline in Free Cash Flow ($3.3B for H1 2024).

• **Forward-Looking Nuance:** Management "expects" (high confidence) U.S. volume growth to continue in 2H but only "anticipates" (moderate confidence) margin improvement, contingent on successful automation of 400+ sorting facilities by 2028.
