• **Asymmetric Regulatory Risk:** A critical structural vulnerability exists at the ComEd segment regarding Nuclear Decommissioning Trust (NDT) funds. While excess NDT balances must be returned to customers (intercompany payable), accounting rules prohibit ComEd from recognizing a regulatory asset for fund shortfalls. This creates a one-way downside risk where market volatility in NDT assets can trigger immediate P&L charges without the possibility of a regulatory buffer.

• **Sentiment Divergence (Decommissioning):** Management expresses "belief" that NDT fund growth will cover future costs, yet the $53 million charge for Byron units indicates a widening gap between long-term projections and current regulatory/contractual realities. The use of "believes" (speculative) regarding fund adequacy contrasts with the "recorded" (realized) nature of the Byron charge.
