* **Sentiment Divergence:** Management raised FY24 guidance (Net Sales +5.0-5.5%; Adj. EPS $6.40-$6.48) but qualitatively flagged "uneven" demand. Critical divergence noted in late October spending patterns: a sharp deceleration in the final 14 days of the quarter contradicts the headline 4.9% U.S. SSS beat, signaling a bearish entry into Q4.
* **Hidden Operational Shifts:** Inventory levels decreased 1.2% YoY to $63.3B despite 5.2% revenue growth, indicating high sell-through efficiency and aggressive destocking of discretionary goods. Accounts Payable fell to $58.1B from $59.0B at fiscal year-end, suggesting a contraction in supplier-financed float or tactical shifts in procurement timing. 
* **Supply Chain/R&D Pivot:** Accelerated CapEx toward "Next Generation" fulfillment; management targets ~55% of fulfillment center volume to be automated by FY26, projecting a 20% unit cost reduction. This shifts R&D focus from consumer tech to industrial robotics/AI-driven logistics.
* **Segment KPI Trajectory:** 
    *   **Walmart U.S.:** SSS +4.9% (ex-fuel); Transactions +3.4%; Ticket +1.5%. 
    *   **eCommerce:** Global growth +15%, U.S. +24%. High-margin "Walmart Connect" ad revenue +26% YoY, becoming a primary driver of operating margin expansion (OP inc +3.0% vs Sales +5.2%).
* **Capital Allocation:** Repurchased 8.2M shares in Q3 for $1.3B ($158.53 avg price); $15.4B remains under current authorization. Interest expense rose to $630M (+22% YoY), reflecting higher rates on floating-debt tranches despite $1.5B in senior note issuance.
* **Risk Factors:** Modified focus on "deflationary risks" in general merchandise. Management explicitly noted that if food inflation moderates faster than anticipated, the "ticket" component of SSS will face significant downward pressure, as volume growth (+3.4%) may not compensate for price normalization.
