• **Operational Pivot:** Complete cessation of REGEN-COV U.S. revenue (down to $0 from $2.59B in 1H21) marks a total operational shift back to core franchises. Growth is now entirely dependent on the Sanofi collaboration (Dupixent) and Eylea market share retention.
• **Sentiment Divergence:** Management maintains high-confidence guidance ("we expect") for the Eylea 8mg high-dose launch to defend the franchise, despite the material threat of technology obsolescence/cannibalization from Roche’s Vabysmo and impending biosimilars.
• **Concentration Risk:** Extreme revenue dependency remains technical; three distributors (McKesson, Besse Medical, Cardinal Health) historically represent >90% of U.S. gross product sales. The expiration of US Government contracts for COVID-19 therapies materially alters the accounts receivable aging profile and credit risk.
• **Collaboration KPI Trajectory:** Sanofi collaboration revenue is shifting from R&D reimbursement toward "Share of Profits." This indicates Dupixent has reached a post-break-even maturity stage where REGN captures higher-margin bottom-line flow-through rather than just OpEx offsets.
• **Capital Allocation:** Aggressive buyback acceleration serves as a primary EPS floor. REGN repurchased $597M in stock during Q2 2022 ($1.2B for 1H 2022), signaling a pivot toward returning capital as organic pipeline expansion in oncology (Libtayo) requires less immediate cash than the COVID-era manufacturing scale-up.
• **R&D Spending Pivot:** Significant reallocation of capital toward "Immuno-oncology" collaboration with Sanofi and internal bispecific programs, effectively deprecating the primary antiviral research infrastructure.
