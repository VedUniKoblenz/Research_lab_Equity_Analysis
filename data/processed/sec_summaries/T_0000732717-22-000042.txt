• **Accounting-Driven EPS Tailwinds:** Management extended estimated service lives for technology equipment and elongated amortization periods for customer fulfillment costs effective Q1 22. These non-cash changes provide an immediate, persistent margin cushion that partially masks rising Opex in the 5G rollout phase.
• **RPO Duration Signal:** Remaining Performance Obligations (RPO) show a non-obvious duration mismatch; only 20% is expected to be recognized over the next 21 months. This suggests a significantly long-tail revenue realization profile or potential friction in converting the backlog into immediate top-line growth.
• **Hidden Leverage via Vendor Financing:** Usage of structured vendor financing payables remains a critical liquidity lever, with $2.0B+ in non-current liabilities carrying a 2-year settlement window. This bridges the gap between high CapEx intensity and the Free Cash Flow (FCF) requirements for the reset dividend.
• **WarnerMedia Spinoff Liquidity:** Post-quarter (April 8, 2022), T received $40.4B in cash and debt retention. While deleveraging is the stated goal, the filing reveals significant "stranded costs" and "reconciling items" ($1.2B+ range) related to the Video/DirecTV transition that continue to weigh on the "Communications" segment margins.
• **Segment KPI Trajectory:** 
  - **Mobility:** Shift toward high-value plans is evident, but the 10-Q highlights increased "Securitization Fees" linked to device receivables, signaling higher costs to maintain the current subscriber acquisition pace.
  - **Business Wireline:** Secular decline in legacy voice/data is accelerating; management "anticipates" (moderate confidence) that fiber growth will offset this, but current data shows a widening gap in the transition period.
• **Capital Allocation Reset:** Dividend obligation reduced from ~$15B annually to ~$8B post-spinoff. The pace of debt retirement is now the primary alpha driver, with a target Net Debt-to-Adjusted EBITDA ratio of 2.5x by year-end 2023.
