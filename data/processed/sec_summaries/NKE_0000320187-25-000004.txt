• **Sentiment Divergence:** Management maintains "high confidence" in a multi-year innovation cycle (FY26/27 focus), yet Q2 data shows a 9% YoY revenue contraction to $11.59B. A widening gap exists between bullish "Return to Offense" rhetoric and a 15% decline in NIKE Digital sales, suggesting structural brand heat loss rather than temporary macro headwinds.
• **Operational Shifts:** Significant channel pivot in progress; Wholesale is being re-prioritized to arrest the 10% decline in NIKE Direct. "Save to Invest" restructuring on track for $2B cumulative savings, but efficiency is offset by a 4% increase in Demand Creation Expense ($1.1B), indicating higher CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost) to sustain decelerating traffic. Inventory levels fell 5% to $8.3B, but gross margin expansion (44.5%) was primarily driven by lower ocean freight/logistics costs rather than full-price sell-through.
• **Forward-Looking Nuance:** Specific "We expect" guidance confirms FY25 revenue will decline in the mid-single-digit range. Management "anticipates" (lower confidence) gross margin expansion of 50-60 bps, but highlights "volatility" in Greater China and FX as primary sensitivities.
• **KPI Trajectory:** NIKE Direct revenues $5.4B (-10%); Digital -15%. Footwear segment remains the primary drag with Greater China EBIT down 9% on a currency-neutral basis, signaling ASP (Average Selling Price) compression in the premium performance category.
• **Capital Allocation:** Dividend increased 8% to $0.40/share; $1.2B in share repurchases (15.1M shares) at an average price of ~$79.50, signaling an aggressive valuation floor-setting strategy despite FCF (Free Cash Flow) headwinds.
• **Hidden Risk:** Heavy reliance on "Other Nonoperating Income" for earnings beats; foreign currency hedging gains are currently masking core EBIT erosion. A shift in USD strength poses a material "quality of earnings" risk as hedge layers roll over in H2 FY25.
