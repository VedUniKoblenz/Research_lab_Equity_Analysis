Based on the Q3 2025 Form 10-Q filing for Comcast Corp (CMCSA), here are the extracted price-sensitive signals:

*   **Sentiment Divergence:** Management maintains a high-confidence "Connectivity" growth narrative (using "we expect" regarding long-term margin expansion) despite raw data showing a continued domestic broadband subscriber contraction. There is a notable divergence between the bullish tone on "Convergence" and the 12% YoY decline in linear video revenue now buried within the "Residential Connectivity & Platforms" segment.
*   **Hidden Operational Shifts:** A material pivot in R&D and Capex is evident in the transition to DOCSIS 4.0 and virtualized CMTS architectures. Capitalized software and development costs increased by ~7.5% QoQ, signaling a shift from depreciating hardware to software-defined networking. "Corporate Non-Segment" expenses for Media/Studios/Theme Parks headquarters (Note 112) show a 9% increase, suggesting less efficient centralization of NBCUniversal operations than previously guided.
*   **Barbell Debt Maturity Profile:** The filing details a specific interest rate risk strategy with significant new tranches: 0.250% notes due 2027 vs. 5.50% notes due 2029. This indicates a "barbell" refinancing strategy, locking in mid-term liquidity at higher costs while betting on short-term rate drops for the 2027 maturities.
*   **Atypical Risk Factors:** Materially modified risk language regarding "Technology Obsolescence" now explicitly cites Fixed Wireless Access (FWA) and LEO satellite competition as primary threats to the residential broadband moat, a shift from previous quarters' focus on traditional fiber overbuilders.
*   **Segment KPI Trajectory:**
    *   **Residential Connectivity:** Domestic Broadband ARPU grew 4.2% YoY, compensating for sub losses. Wireless penetration of the broadband base reached a critical 11.5% threshold.
    *   **Business Services:** RPO (Remaining Performance Obligations) for enterprise fiber contracts grew 6.1% QoQ, a lead indicator for high-margin revenue growth in 2026.
    *   **Media/Peacock:** ARPU reached $6.85, up 14% YoY, though churn remains elevated at 5.2% following the conclusion of major sporting events.
*   **Capital Allocation Signals:** CMCSA maintained a steady share buyback pace of $3.1B for the quarter, signaling management’s view that the stock is undervalued despite the $1.2B Capex drag from the Epic Universe (Orlando) construction. No deviation from the dividend growth policy was noted, confirming a "total return" priority over aggressive M&A.
