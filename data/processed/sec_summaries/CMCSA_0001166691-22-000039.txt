• **Broadband Stagnation Signal**: Extreme deceleration in Cable net adds to 14k (vs. 300k YoY), confirming a structural shift toward "flat-to-low" growth. Management tone remains "confident" in long-term penetration despite residential broadband revenue growth slowing to 5.7%, a clear sentiment-data divergence.
• **Wireless Pivot**: Xfinity Mobile net adds hit 333k, with segment revenue up 30.8% to $789M. Wireless is effectively the primary churn-mitigation tool for the 561k Video sub loss, representing a fundamental shift from "Cable-first" to "Connectivity-bundle" economics.
• **NBCU Margin Volatility**: Theme Parks hit record $2.1B revenue (+42.4%) with $819M EBITDA, but this is offset by Media segment EBITDA compression (-41.6% YoY). Peacock paid subs reached 15M, yet the platform realized a $614M adjusted EBITDA loss in Q3 alone, totaling $1.8B for 9M22.
• **FX & Sky Impairment Risk**: Sky revenue fell 14.7% to $4.3B (flat on CC basis), signaling severe USD headwind. Management "expects" further macro-driven pressure in Europe; the $8.6B goodwill balance for Sky remains a significant impairment risk factor if the UK/EU consumer environment continues to deteriorate.
• **Capital Allocation Aggression**: Buyback pace accelerated to $3.5B in Q3 ($9.5B YTD), representing ~7.5% of market cap on an annualized basis. This aggressive pace suggests a management "floor" on the stock price despite weakening broadband KPIs.
• **Operational Shift**: Capital expenditures increased 14.1% to $2.6B, driven by "Next-Generation" network investment (DOCSIS 4.0), indicating high-confidence spending to combat Fixed Wireless Access (FWA) competition.
