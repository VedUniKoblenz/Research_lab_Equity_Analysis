• Global comparable sales growth decelerated sharply to 1.9% in Q1 2024 vs. 12.6% in Q1 2023, signaling a significant exhaustion of pricing power and consumer elasticity limits.
• IDL (International Developmental Licensed) segment turned negative at -0.2% comp sales, a direct result of Middle East geopolitical conflict, representing a localized but material geographic risk pivot.
• Management tone remains bullish on "long-term resilience," yet raw data shows U.S. comparable guest counts were negative for the quarter, masked only by increased Average Order Value (AOV).
• Restructuring tail: $27.3M remaining liability for "Special Termination Benefits" indicates the 2023 organizational overhaul ("Accelerating the Arches") is still incurring cash outflows and hasn't fully stabilized headcount.
• Supply chain/Inventory: No material write-downs, but accounts payable surged to $1.1B, suggesting aggressive working capital management to offset slowing top-line cash flow.
• Guidance Nuance: "We expect" 2024 CapEx of $2.5B–$2.7B to fund ~2,100 gross openings; the reliance on unit growth (4% net new restaurant contribution) to drive systemwide sales suggests a pivot away from organic same-store volume.
• FX Hedging: Utilizing $4.3B in foreign-currency-denominated debt as net investment hedges; significant sensitivity to Euro and GBP fluctuations remains a primary non-operational EPS mover.
• Capital Allocation: $553.8M in share repurchases in Q1; maintaining pace despite $37.5B in total debt, signaling a commitment to floor the stock price via buybacks despite rising interest expense ($315M).
