• **Top-Line Divergence:** Q2 Net Sales of $94.8B (-3% YoY) masks sharp hardware bifurcation. iPhone revenue hit a March quarter record of $51.3B (+2% YoY) despite macroeconomic softening, contrasting with catastrophic Mac contraction to $7.2B (-31% YoY) and iPad decline to $6.7B (-13% YoY). 

• **Inventory Liquidation:** Aggressive inventory reduction to $7.48B (down from $12.8B in Q1; -42% QoQ) suggests rapid clearing of excess channel stock and supply chain normalization post-COVID disruptions. 

• **R&D Pivot:** R&D expenditure scaled to $7.58B (+10% YoY) despite a -3% revenue decline. This identifies a strategic decoupling of long-term project funding (AI/Spatial Computing) from current-period sales performance.

• **Services KPI:** Services revenue reached an all-time record of $20.9B. Performance Obligation (RPO) data indicates 65% of current deferred revenue will be recognized within 12 months, signaling high visibility into high-margin ARR.

• **Geographic Alpha:** Rest of Asia Pacific emerged as the primary growth engine ($8.1B, +15% YoY), providing a significant offset to the -8% contraction in the Americas ($37.8B) and -3% in Greater China ($17.8B).

• **Capital Allocation Surge:** Board authorized a massive $90B increase to the share repurchase program. Dividend raised 4% to $0.24/share, reinforcing a "net cash neutral" goal despite $166B in total debt.

• **Guidance Nuance:** Management expects June quarter YoY revenue performance to trend similar to March (-3%), with a 400bps FX headwind. Speculative "we believe" language used regarding long-term emerging market penetration vs. "we expect" for near-term gross margins (44%-44.5%).
