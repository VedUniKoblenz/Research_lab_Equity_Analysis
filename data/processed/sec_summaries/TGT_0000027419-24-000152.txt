• **KPI Trajectory:** Q2 Comparable Sales +2.0%, marking the first positive turn in five quarters. Growth driven by Traffic (+3.0%) but offset by Average Ticket (-1.0%), signaling deflationary pressure or lower-value basket mix. Digital Comps +8.7% significantly outperformed Physical Store Comps (+0.7%), with same-day services (Drive Up/Target Circle 360) growing 11%.

• **Sentiment Divergence:** Management maintains a bullish "efficiency" narrative, yet SG&A expenses rose to $5.3B (20.8% of sales) vs $5.1B (20.6% YoY). Net income growth (+42.7%) was almost entirely fueled by 190 bps of Gross Margin expansion (to 28.9%)—driven by lower markdowns and supply chain costs—rather than sustainable top-line organic store performance.

• **Hidden Operational Shifts:** Inventory ended at $12.6B, down 0.7% YoY, indicating an aggressive lean-stocking stance. Discretionary categories (Apparel) showed a pivot to positive comps (+3%), while "Household Essentials" and "Food & Beverage" stabilized. 

• **Forward-Looking Nuance:** Management "expects" (high confidence) FY24 GAAP/Adjusted EPS of $9.00–$9.70, a material upward revision from the prior $8.60–$9.60 range. Q3 comparable sales are "expected" in a range of 0%–2%.

• **Capital Allocation:** Re-initiation of share repurchases; $155M deployed in Q2 (1.1M shares at ~$145.19 avg) following zero activity in Q1. Dividend increased 1.8% to $1.12 per share. $9.5B remains under current buyback authorization.

• **Risk Factors:** Modified focus on "value-conscious" consumer behavior; price-cut strategy (applied to 5,000+ items) is a defensive response to competitive landscape shifts. Regulatory risk highlighted regarding CFPB credit card late fee caps, threatening the "Other Revenue" segment.
