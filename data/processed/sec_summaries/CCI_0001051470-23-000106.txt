• **Capital Allocation Surprise:** Post-quarter (April 26, 2023), CCI issued $1.35B in senior unsecured notes in two tranches: $600M at 4.800% (2028) and $750M at 5.100% (2033). This marks a significant pivot from the 1.05%–2.25% coupons seen in 2020-2021, signaling a permanent step-up in cost of capital that will compress AFFO as older debt matures.
• **Hidden Operational Shift:** Accelerated migration to SOFR from LIBOR for the $7.0B revolving credit facility and $3.0B term loans. The implementation of a "Term SOFR Adjustment" (10bps to 25bps) adds a hidden layer of interest expense on top of base rate hikes, contributing to the $128M YoY increase in interest expense for Q1.
• **Segment KPI Trajectory:** Tower segment organic growth remains high at ~5%, but Fiber segment performance is clouded by the Sprint/T-Mobile churn. Management "expects" $200M in 2023 revenue impact specifically tied to site non-renewals, with the majority concentrated in the high-margin Fiber/Small Cell footprint.
• **Forward-Looking Nuance:** Management used "expect" (high confidence) regarding the deployment of 10,000 small cell nodes in 2023, but switched to "anticipate" regarding the timing of co-location on existing fiber, suggesting potential delays in the 50,000+ node backlog realization.
• **Sentiment Divergence:** Management maintained a bullish tone on 5G densification despite a marked decline in "Network services and other" revenue (down to $166M from $185M YoY), indicating that carrier capex is tightening faster than narrative suggests.
• **Restructuring Signals:** Asset write-downs and "prepaid site ground lease" amortization ($18M) suggest a strategic move to secure land under towers to mitigate long-term OpEx, though at the cost of immediate cash liquidity.
• **Debt Maturity Wall:** Concentration of maturities in the 2023-2025 window requires approximately $3.5B in refinancing; the jump to >5% rates on the April issuance serves as a bearish lead-indicator for dividend growth sustainability.
