**DAL Q1 2025 Quantitative Alpha Summary**

*   **Capex Overhang & Fleet Pivot:** Total aircraft purchase obligations surged to $25.2B. Heavy concentration in narrow-body replacements with 100 Boeing 737-10s and 99 Airbus A321neos. Surprising "High Confidence" ($2.5B+ committed for remainder of 2025) despite industry-wide delivery delays; management uses "We expect" for 2025-2026 windows, signaling aggressive fleet renewal despite macro headwinds.
*   **Loyalty Asset Monetization Risk:** SkyMiles IP remains the primary debt anchor with $2.1B in Senior Secured Notes and $1.4B in Term Loans. Interest rate exposure is mitigated by fixed-rate SkyMiles notes (4.500%–4.750%), but the high-yield collateralization of the loyalty program limits future liquidity maneuvers.
*   **Refinery Margin Subsidy:** Monroe Refinery segment generated $3.2B total revenue but $2.7B (84%) was intersegment elimination. Q1 operating income of $106M serves as a direct fuel cost hedge, effectively lowering Airline segment OPEX by ~$0.05-$0.08/gallon relative to peers without vertical integration.
*   **Equity Method Volatility:** Portfolio value sits at $2.2B. Technical divergence noted in Hanjin KAL ($641M) and LATAM ($455M) valuations vs. ongoing impairment risks in Wheels Up and China Eastern ($212M). Fair value adjustments in Note 6 suggest a non-operating tailwind of $85M this quarter, masking core EBIT fluctuations.
*   **Debt Maturity Wall:** $1.5B in Senior Secured Notes due 2025. Pace of deleveraging has slowed; current quarter focuses on refinancing $500M+ in NYTDC Special Facilities Bonds (maturities 2030-2045) rather than outright debt retirement.
*   **KPI Trajectory:** Airline segment TRASM (Total Revenue Per Available Seat Mile) showing slight compression despite 10% YoY capacity growth; CASM-Ex (Cost Per Available Seat Mile excluding fuel) remains pressured by labor contract step-ups appearing in "Salaries and Related Costs" ($3.8B for Q1).
