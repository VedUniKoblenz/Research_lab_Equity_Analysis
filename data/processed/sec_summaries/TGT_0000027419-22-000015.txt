• **Inventory-to-Sales Mismatch:** Q1 inventory surged to $15.08B, a +43.1% YoY increase, drastically outstripping total sales growth of 4.0%. This divergence signals a massive overstock in discretionary "Hardlines" and "Home" segments, portending severe margin-dilutive liquidations in Q2/Q3.
• **Aggressive Capital Allocation Divergence:** Despite Net Cash from Operations turning negative (-$1.45B vs. +$1.05B YoY), management executed $2.1B in share repurchases at an average price of $211.58. The maintenance of the 2022 Repurchase Program amid a liquidity drain suggests over-optimism or a failure to pivot to capital preservation.
• **Funding & Rate Risk:** Commercial paper outstandings spiked to $1.1B from zero in the prior year, indicating a sudden reliance on short-term credit markets to fund working capital/inventory bloat. TGT entered $2.5B in notional forward-starting interest rate swaps, signaling high-confidence internal projections of sustained hawkish macro conditions.
• **Margin Compression Nuance:** Gross margin rate collapsed to 25.7% (down from 30.0% YoY). Management "expects" incremental supply chain costs of $1.1B for the full year, but the "anticipate" language regarding operating margin recovery in H2 is speculative given the current 5.3% operating margin (down from 9.8%).
• **Segment KPI Trajectory:** Digital SSS grew 3.2%, a sharp deceleration from pandemic comps. Crucially, "Drive Up" grew 18%, increasing fulfillment complexity and labor touchpoints as a percentage of GMV, further squeezing unit economics.
• **Hidden Restructuring:** Total SG&A rose 6.7% to $4.9B, driven largely by "investments in compensation," signaling a permanent upward shift in the labor cost floor that cannot be easily offloaded if traffic (currently +3.9%) softens.
