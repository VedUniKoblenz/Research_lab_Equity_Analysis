Significant operational shift: $300M allocated to severance/termination benefits and $300M for real estate/office space consolidation. Signal: Shift from aggressive growth to margin defense.

• **Segment Divergence Alpha:** Severe performance decoupling between core and growth silos. Secure, Agile Networks surged 12% YoY ($6.68B) as backlog cleared, but "Internet for the Future" (hyperscale/optical) contracted 5% to $1.30B. Collaboration remained a drag, down 2% to $1.09B.

• **RPO & Backlog Quality:** Total RPO hit $30.9B (+3% YoY). Critical metric: 52% ($16.1B) is current (next 12 months). Product RPO ($16.4B) now exceeds Service RPO ($14.5B), confirming a successful transition toward software-heavy product cycles despite hardware delivery lags.

• **Working Capital Friction:** Inventory remained flat at $2.56B, but "Unbilled Receivables" increased to $1.15B (vs. $1.04B at fiscal year-end), suggesting complex multi-year contract structures are delaying cash conversion despite revenue recognition.

• **Capital Allocation Aggression:** Repurchased 28M shares for $1.25B in Q1 (avg price $43.51). When combined with $1.57B in dividends, total capital return ($2.82B) consumed nearly 71% of the $3.95B in Q1 operating cash flow.

• **Credit Risk Nuance:** Finance receivables (gross) stood at $8.86B. While 98% are "Grade 1-4" (investment grade), "Grade 7 and Higher" (high risk) receivables increased by 14% since fiscal year-end, signaling emerging credit stress in the customer base.
