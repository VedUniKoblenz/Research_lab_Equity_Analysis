• **Sentiment Divergence**: Management maintains long-term "Accelerating the Arches" bullishness despite a -1.0% global comparable sales contraction—the first quarterly decline since Q2 2020. Internal "value perception" concerns now openly contradict previous "pricing power" narratives.
• **KPI Deceleration**: U.S. same-store sales (SSS) flipped to -0.7% (from +10.3% YoY), confirming a negative traffic trend that price-led average check growth can no longer mask. 
• **IDL Segment Fragility**: IDL SSS of -1.3% (vs. +14.0% YoY) reflects a structural, non-transitory impact from the Middle East conflict. The acquisition of the 225-unit Israeli licensee (Alonyal Limited) signals a forced re-consolidation to prevent developmental licensee insolvency.
• **Forward-Looking Nuance**: Management "expects" (high confidence) full-year 2024 operating margins in the mid-to-high 40% range, but only "anticipates" (moderate) that consumer pressures will persist for several quarters, suggesting a potential margin-trapping scenario if value wars escalate.
• **Atypical Risk Escalation**: New emphasis on "brand perception risk" tied to international geopolitical volatility and a widening "value gap" against QSR peers, necessitating unbudgeted marketing spend for the $5 Meal Deal extension.
• **Capital Allocation Shift**: Despite softening fundamentals, 2024 CapEx guidance is maintained at $2.5B–$2.7B to support 2,100+ gross unit openings, prioritizing footprint expansion over immediate SSS recovery.
• **Operational Restructuring**: Q2 recognized $35M in pre-tax restructuring charges, indicating ongoing corporate headcount rationalization and G&A tightening to offset store-level labor inflation.
