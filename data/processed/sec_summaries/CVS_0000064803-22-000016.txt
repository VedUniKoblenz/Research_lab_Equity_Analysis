• **Capital Allocation Pivot:** Resumed common stock repurchases in Q1 2022, totaling $2.0B (approx. 19.1M shares), signaling a definitive end to the post-Aetna deleveraging phase and a shift toward aggressive shareholder returns.
• **Guidance Upside:** FY2022 Adjusted EPS guidance raised to $8.20–$8.40 (from $8.10–$8.30); GAAP EPS floor raised to $7.04. Projected cash flow from operations remains high at $12.0B–$13.0B.
• **Sentiment Divergence:** Management maintains bullishness on "integrated health" synergies despite a $484M settlement cost for Florida opioid litigation; the divergence lies in raising guidance while simultaneously absorbing material legal hits.
• **Health Care Benefits (HCB):** MBR (Medical Benefit Ratio) improved to 83.5% (YoY decrease of 30 bps), driven by lower COVID-19 related costs and favorable prior-year reserve development, countering inflationary pressure in labor.
• **Pharmacy Services KPI:** Total pharmacy claims processed rose 5.8% to 580.4M; net new business wins for 2022 totaling $8.9B in annual contract value.
• **Retail/LTC Trajectory:** Same-store sales increased 10.7%; Front-store same-store sales surged 13.2%, heavily indexed to COVID-19 OTC test kit demand—a high-margin but non-recurring "cliff" risk for H2.
• **Operational Shift:** Inventory levels increased to $18.3B (+2.8% since YE21), reflecting defensive stockpiling against global supply chain volatility and inflation.
• **Debt/Liquidity:** Net repayment of long-term debt totaled $2.1B in Q1; total debt-to-capital ratio remains stable at approx. 48%, maintaining IG rating headroom for potential primary care M&A.
