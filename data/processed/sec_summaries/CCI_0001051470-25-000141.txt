This signals the imminent divestiture of the fiber/small cell segment, effectively ending CCI’s dual-track strategy.
• **Layered Restructuring:** Adoption of a new "2024 Restructuring Plan" (Context c-157) on top of the existing 2023 Plan. Ongoing Q1 2025 charges for employee severance and facility closures (Context c-161, c-162) indicate a deeper, sustained push to strip opex beyond initial guidance.
• **Cost of Debt Escalation:** Significant debt stack migration from 2020/2021 legacy rates (1.35%–2.25%) to "August 2024 Senior Unsecured Notes" at 4.900% and 5.200% (Context c-80, c-96). The lack of 2025 refinancing at lower rates suggests a structural shift in interest expense that will pressure AFFO per share.
• **Liquidity Dependency:** Increased reliance on short-term funding through Commercial Paper (Context c-54) and Revolving Credit Facilities (Context c-123) to bridge the gap during the asset-held-for-sale transition period.
• **Segment KPI Shift:** Trajectory focus has shifted entirely to "Continuing Operations" (Tower segment) (Context c-136). The separation of "Discontinued Operations" (Context c-134) implies a 100% pivot to a pure-play tower model, prioritizing site rental growth and decommissioning high-capex fiber obligations.
• **Capital Allocation:** Dividend maintenance for Q1 2025 (Context c-127) despite the massive shift to "Held for Sale" status indicates management is prioritizing REIT compliance/yield over deleveraging during the restructuring phase.
