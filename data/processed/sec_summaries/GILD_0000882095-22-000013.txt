• **GAAP Operating Income Compression:** Collapsed to $175M (vs. $2.9B YoY) driven by a $2.7B non-cash impairment charge of In-Process R&D (IPR&D) related to Trodelvy. This signals a material downward revision of internal valuation/ROI for the $21B Immunomedics acquisition following clinical data re-assessments.
• **Veklury Concentration Risk:** $1.54B in Q1 revenue (23% of total) was COVID-dependent. Management "expects" Veklury revenue to be highly volatile and transition to a secondary driver, yet it currently bridges the gap for stagnant core HIV/HCV growth.
• **HIV Segment KPI Divergence:** Biktarvy revenue grew 18% YoY to $2.15B, yet total HIV segment growth was muted at 2% ($3.7B). Signal: Rapid cannibalization of older TAF-based internal products (Descovy, Genvoya) and 340B pricing pressures are offsetting volume gains; US Biktarvy market share now 44%.
• **Oncology Pivot Acceleration:** Cell Therapy (Yescarta/Tecartus) revenue jumped 43% YoY to $265M. Trodelvy grew 103% YoY to $146M. R&D spend is aggressively shifting toward these high-margin, biologics-based segments to mitigate small-molecule LOE (Loss of Exclusivity) risks.
• **Capital Allocation Shifts:** Repaid $500M in 3.25% Senior Notes; repurchased only $352M in common stock (approx. 5M shares). Signal: Shift toward debt reduction and dividend maintenance ($945M paid) over aggressive buybacks amidst IPR&D write-downs.
• **Regulatory/Technology Risk:** Formalized risk language regarding "clinical holds" following Magrolimab (CD47) partial holds and Lenacapavir CRL (Complete Response Letter) due to vial chemistry. This creates a technical bottleneck for GILD’s "long-acting" HIV strategy, increasing dependency on daily oral Biktarvy.
• **Inventory/AR Nuance:** Accounts Receivable decreased to $4.2B (from $5.1B at YE21) while Inventories rose to $3.1B. This inventory build suggests management "anticipates" supply chain volatility or is front-loading for H2 oncology launches.
