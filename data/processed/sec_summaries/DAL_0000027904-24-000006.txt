• **Pacific Revenue Trajectory:** Q1 revenue surged to $526M vs $377M YoY (+39.5%), significantly outpacing Domestic (+5.5%) and Atlantic (+7.6%) growth, signaling a high-alpha pivot toward Asian market recovery.

• **Refinery Revenue Collapse:** Third-party refinery sales plummeted to $725M from $1.1B YoY (-34%), a non-obvious drag on the "Product/Service Other" segment despite management's focus on fuel cost stability.
• **Capital Commitment Escalation:** Jan 2024 execution of an agreement for 20 A350-1000 widebody aircraft; total remaining aircraft purchase obligations now stand at $19.4B, effectively equaling the company's total current debt load.
• **Fleet Concentration Risk:** Backlog is heavily skewed toward two types: A321-200neo (123 units) and B737-10 (100 units), representing 78.5% of the 284-aircraft total commitment and elevating vulnerability to specific OEM/regulatory delays.
• **Deleveraging Velocity:** Management reduced total debt and finance lease obligations by $716M in a single quarter (from $20.1B to $19.4B), signaling aggressive cash flow diversion to the balance sheet over shareholder returns.
• **Investment Concentration:** Strategic equity portfolio ($1.56B total) is concentrated in Hanjin KAL ($492M) and Wheels Up ($330M), representing 53% of non-consolidated asset value.
