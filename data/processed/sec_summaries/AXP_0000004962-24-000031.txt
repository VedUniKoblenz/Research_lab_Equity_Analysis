- **Credit Quality Inflection:** Consumer loan net write-off rates (principal only) surged to 2.3% in Q1'24 from 1.7% YoY; Small Business (SME) write-offs accelerated more aggressively, jumping to 2.2% from 1.4% YoY, signaling a rapid erosion in the small-cap commercial tier.
- **Negative Credit Migration:** Total loans 30-89 days past due increased to $1.31B from $1.25B sequentially (Q4'23). Notably, the 90+ day bucket rose to $789M, indicating that normalization is transitioning into structural credit stress despite the "premium" cardmember profile.
- **Asset Mix Shift:** Total Cardmember Receivables (transactor-heavy) contracted 10.3% sequentially to $56.9B, while Cardmember Loans (revolving/interest-bearing) grew to $122.9B. This pivot toward revolving balances increases Interest Income exposure but correlates with the rising Net Write-Off trajectory.
- **SME Segment Vulnerability:** SME net write-offs (including principal, interest, and fees) hit 2.8% in Q1'24 vs 1.9% YoY. This 90bps delta suggests macroeconomic pressure is hitting the AXP commercial base harder than the individual consumer.
- **Liquidity/Funding Nuance:** Reliance on Variable Interest Entities (VIEs) remains high, with $20.9B in Cardmember Loans and $4.8B in Receivables now consolidated in VIEs to support securitization-led funding.
- **Capital Allocation:** Common Equity Tier 1 (CET1) capital remains stable, but the dividend on Series D Preferred Stock consumed material cash flow ($26M for the quarter), while common stock par value remains at $0.20.
- **Sentiment Divergence:** Management continues to emphasize high-spend transactors, yet the $1.1B sequential increase in interest-bearing loan balances suggests a shift toward cardmembers utilizing AXP as a credit bridge rather than a pure payment rail.
