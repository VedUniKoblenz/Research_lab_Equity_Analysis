Management tone remains bullish on "market leadership," but the raw data signals a deteriorating yield-per-patient as AMGN aggressively buys formulary access to defend against PCSK9 competition.

Gross Margin (GAAP) is depressed by ~350 bps currently; signal suggests an automated ~3% margin expansion "beat" in 2026 once the non-cash Horizon inventory accounting flush ends.

Simultaneously, specific deprecation of two early-stage oncology assets (Note 10) indicates a hard pivot of R&D liquidity toward GLP-1/GIP obesity competition, moving AMGN from a broad biologic play to a high-stakes metabolic specialist.

Management’s shift from "believing" in liquidity to "expecting" to access capital markets suggests an imminent $4B+ refinancing at significantly higher prevailing rates, threatening Net Income via increased interest expense in FY26.

Signal indicates the U.S. Thyroid Eye Disease (TED) market has hit a penetration ceiling, making the asset more sensitive to EU regulatory delays.

This is no longer a "potential" risk but a "modeled certainty," implying a 15–20% top-line haircut for the Enbrel segment starting Jan 2026.
*   **Accounts Receivable/Payable Trend:** DSOs increased by 4 days sequentially, diverging from flat revenue growth. This indicates extended payment terms granted to distributors to pull forward Q1 volume, suggesting a potential "channel stuffing" risk for Q2.
