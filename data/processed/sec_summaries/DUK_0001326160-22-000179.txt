• **RPO Realization Velocity:** XBRL data identifies $5.4B+ in Remaining Performance Obligations (RPO) with a compressed 9-month satisfaction window for the primary tranche. This indicates higher-than-consensus revenue conversion velocity for the remainder of FY2022.
• **Fuel Cost Working Capital Drag:** Significant divergence between bullish O&M "agility" narrative and raw "Fuel and purchased power" expense growth. Rising commodity prices are inflating deferred fuel regulatory assets, creating a temporary liquidity headwind and a "hidden" debt-like drag of several hundred million dollars until rate recovery cycles catch up.
• **Segment-Specific Load Shift:** Carolinas and Florida nodes showing 1.8%–2.1% organic customer growth. However, a technical shift is occurring: residential load is normalizing downward post-pandemic while commercial/industrial recovery (up ~3% YoY) is failing to fully offset the margin-rich residential drop-off on a per-kWh basis.
• **Supply Chain Vulnerability:** Materially modified risk factor regarding solar auxiliary equipment and panel procurement. Anti-circumvention inquiries and UFLPA enforcement represent a high-materiality threat to the $63B five-year CAPEX plan, specifically the 2022-2023 renewable deployment targets.
• **Capital Allocation Refinement:** Management maintains 2022 EPS guidance ($5.30–$5.60) and 5%–7% long-term CAGR, but reliance on new debt issuance in a rising-rate environment suggests a narrowing spread between ROE and WACC, potentially pressuring the 65%–75% dividend payout target by 2024.
• **Operational Pivot:** Accelerated depreciation of coal-fired assets is being offset by a pivot to grid modernization and battery storage R&D spending, shifting the rate-base growth engine from generation to transmission/distribution.
