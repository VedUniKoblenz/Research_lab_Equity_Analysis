• **Sentiment/Data Divergence:** Management maintains "all-weather" bullishness despite a GAAP Operating Margin collapse to 18.9% (from 30.7% YoY). Contraction is masked by non-GAAP adjustments; divergence stems from a $765M impairment of the BodyArmor trademark and $1.9B in charges related to refranchising bottling operations.
• **Volume/Pricing Imbalance:** Top-line growth of 13% price/mix vs. only 1% unit case volume growth. North America volume was 0% (flat), signaling exhaustion of pricing power in the core market. EMEA growth (2%) was heavily offset by geopolitical tension in the Middle East.
• **Hidden Operational Shifts:** Inventory rose to $4.82B (+9% from FY23) despite flat volumes, suggesting potential overstock or supply chain friction. Accounts Payable decreased by $1.1B, indicating aggressive cash outflows to suppliers.
• **Tax Litigation Escalation:** Material update on IRS transfer pricing dispute; potential liability now quantified at ~$16B (including interest). KO noted it is "more likely than not" to prevail, but the scale of potential cash outflow represents 60%+ of annual EBITDA.
• **Forward-Looking Nuance:** "We expect" 8–9% organic revenue growth (upgraded from 6–7%), but "anticipate" a high-single-digit currency headwind (8–9%) on comparable net revenues, effectively neutralizing the organic beat.
• **Segment KPIs:** Latin America remains the primary engine (Unit Case Volume +4%, Price/Mix +22%). North America is the primary drag (Concentrate sales -2%). 
• **Capital Allocation:** Share buybacks totaled $235M in Q1, a relatively slow pace vs. the $1.7B FY24 target. Debt levels remain elevated with $3B in new notes issued at 4.900%–5.375%, significantly higher than the 1.100%–1.875% maturing tranches.
