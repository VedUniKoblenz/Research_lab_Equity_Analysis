• **New Product Portfolio Trajectory:** Combined revenue for new brands (Reblozyl, Zeposia, Abecma, Breyanzi) reached $450M. Reblozyl (+39% to $156M) and Abecma ($67M) are the primary alpha drivers, but the ramp must accelerate 3x to offset Revlimid’s decline.

• **Supply Chain Vulnerability:** Abraxane revenue collapsed to $222M (from $328M) due to "manufacturing delays" at a third-party facility. This signal suggests concentration risk in CMO dependencies for the sterile injectables segment.
• **Capital Allocation Surprise:** Executed a $5.0B Accelerated Share Repurchase (ASR) in Q1. Significant pace increase compared to FY21, signaling management’s view of deep valuation discount during the LOE (Loss of Exclusivity) transition. 
• **Debt/Hedging Pivot:** Issued $6.0B in Senior Notes to refinance $4.0B in near-term debt. Large increase in "Treasury Lock" derivatives ($3.1B notional) suggests aggressive hedging against the 2022 rate hike cycle.
• **Integration Tail-End:** Celgene integration charges fell to $151M, signaling the shift from "merger synergy" to "operational execution" mode.
• **Guidance Nuance:** Management shifted from "anticipate" to "expect" (high confidence) regarding mid-single-digit CAGR through 2025, despite the Revlimid cliff.
