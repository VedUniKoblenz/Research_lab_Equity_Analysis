• **Value-Added Services (VAS) Inflection:** VAS revenue hit $2.49B in Q1’26, a 20.3% YoY increase, significantly outpacing core Service Revenue (+7.5% YoY) and Data Processing (+10.9% YoY). VAS now represents 22.9% of total net revenue, signaling a structural shift toward higher-margin, non-volume-dependent streams.
• **Incentive Intensity:** Client incentives rose to $3.58B, representing 24.8% of gross revenues ($14.44B). This is a 10bps expansion in incentive intensity YoY, suggesting a tightening competitive environment for network exclusivity renewals.
• **International Outperformance:** International Transaction Revenue grew 9.3% YoY to $3.29B, while US-domiciled revenue grew 8.9% to $4.87B. Total International revenue ($5.99B) now accounts for 55.2% of total net revenue, increasing macroeconomic exposure to non-USD currency volatility.
• **Litigation Escrow Depletion:** U.S. Litigation Escrow balance declined by $103M during the quarter (from $1.862B to $1.759B). This drawdown indicates accelerating settlement velocity for opt-out merchant claims or interchange-related liabilities.
• **RPO Recognition Tail:** Remaining Performance Obligations (RPO) show a 50/50 split between <24 months and >24 months. Management projects 50% of current RPO will not be recognized until 2028 or later, indicating increasing contract durations in enterprise and processing segments.
• **Capital Allocation Acceleration:** Declared quarterly dividend increased to $0.59/share (Jan 27, 2026). Senior Notes totaling $20.4B remain stable, but the current portion of long-term debt includes the Dec 2025 notes ($2.25B), signaling a near-term refinancing or cash-settlement event.
• **Asset-Light Operational Profile:** Total assets remained flat at $88.5B despite revenue growth, driving incremental Return on Assets (ROA) expansion as processing volumes scale on fixed infrastructure.
