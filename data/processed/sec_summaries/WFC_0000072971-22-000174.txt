• **NII/NIM Trajectory**: 2Q22 Net Interest Income (NII) surged 16% YoY to $10.2B, driven by higher rates and loan growth (+11% YoY). Management signals high confidence ("we expect") in FY2022 NII growth of ~20% over FY2021, yet 2Q22 non-interest income collapsed 25% to $6.8B, masking core operational volatility.
• **Asset-Liability Mismatch**: AOCI (Accumulated Other Comprehensive Income) realized an $8.1B net loss in 1H22, primarily rate-driven valuation declines in the Available-for-Sale (AFS) portfolio. This significantly compresses tangible book value despite stable CET1 ratios.
• **Mortgage Retrenchment**: Mortgage originations crashed 35% QoQ to $34.1B. Hidden restructuring signal: Headcount reduced by 4,300 in 2Q22; management pivot toward "strategic simplified" mortgage model suggests continued deprecation of third-party origination channels.
• **Credit Normalization Divergence**: Management tone remains bullish on consumer health, yet Provision for Credit Losses reflects a $580M build, including a $235M increase in allowance for credit losses (ACL) for loans. Net charge-offs (0.15%) are at historic lows, but management "anticipates" normalization to accelerate in 2H22.
• **Capital Allocation Brake**: Dividend increased 20% to $0.30/share; however, share buybacks were suspended for 3Q22 to manage capital toward the new 10.3% CET1 requirement (inclusive of 3.2% Stress Capital Buffer).
• **Efficiency & Litigation Tail**: 2Q22 non-interest expense rose 3% QoQ to $12.9B. Litigation accruals remain a "black box" with reasonably possible losses exceeding reserves by $3.9B.
• **KPI Focus**: Commercial Banking NIM expanded 36bps QoQ; Corporate & Investment Banking (CIB) revenue fell 10% YoY, signaling vulnerability to capital market volatility.
