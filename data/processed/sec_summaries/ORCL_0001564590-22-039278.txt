• **Asset Quality & Amortization:** Aggressive amortization schedules for Cerner-related intangibles: Developed Technology (4-year life) and Cloud/License Support relationships (7-year life). This will create a sustained GAAP earnings headwind through 2026 despite operating cash flow strength.
• **Segment KPI Trajectory:** RPO (Remaining Performance Obligation) is heavily concentrated in a 24-month satisfaction window (P2Y), suggesting a shortening of contract durations or a pivot toward mid-term cloud renewals rather than traditional long-term 5-year license tails.
• **Interest Rate Sensitivity:** Material exposure to SOFR/Base Rate fluctuations via the Term Loan Credit Agreement; management utilized interest rate swaps to hedge, but the transition from LIBOR to SOFR remains a friction point for the $15.7B facility.
• **Sentiment Divergence:** Management maintains a "Hyper-growth" narrative for OCI, yet capital expenditure and hardware segment indicators show supply chain constraints and a shift toward "Servers" with a limited 3-5 year useful life, indicating high recurring CapEx requirements to maintain cloud parity.
